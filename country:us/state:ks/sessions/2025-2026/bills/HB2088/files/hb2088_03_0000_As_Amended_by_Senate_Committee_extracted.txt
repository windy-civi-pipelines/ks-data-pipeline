12 New Section 1. (a) The provisions of sections 1 through 3, and
13 amendments thereto, shall be known and may be cited as the fast-track
14 permits act.
15 (b) The purpose of the fast-track permits act is to enhance economic
16 growth in local communities and reduce the regulatory burden on
17 entrepreneurs, developers and homeowners by streamlining the review
18 process for local building permits.

20 (a) "Act" means the fast-track permits act, sections 1 through 3, and

23 government, including a person designated to act on the applicant's behalf.

20 authorization, waiver or denial, as determined by the secretary, to the
21 applicant. The provisions of this section shall not be construed to
22 supersede conflicting federal law.

16 (a) Any governing body which that has enacted a zoning ordinance or

22 city's limits. The members first appointed shall serve respectively for terms
23 of one, two and three years, divided equally or as nearly equally as

31 with the provisions of the ordinance or resolution creating the board.
32 Meetings of the board shall be held at the call of the chairperson and at
33 such other times as the board may determine. The board shall keep
34 minutes of its proceedings, showing evidence presented, findings of fact
35 by the board, decisions of the board and the vote upon each question.
36 Records of all official actions of the board shall be filed in its office and
37 shall be a public record. The governing body, in the ordinance or
38 resolution creating such board, may establish a scale of reasonable fees to
39 be paid in advance by the party appealing. Any two or more cities or
40 counties which that have established a joint planning commission may
41 establish a joint board of zoning appeals.
