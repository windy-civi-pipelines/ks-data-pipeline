Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Education
1-21
1 AN ACT concerning education; relating to school districts; boards of
2 education; requiring attendance center needs assessments to be
3 conducted by the board and include input from board members,
4 teachers, school site councils and administrators; requiring that board
5 members receive certain state assessment data and identify allocations
6 of moneys in the district budget and budget summary; amending
7 K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 72-1163 and repealing the existing section.
8
9 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
10 Section 1. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 72-1163 is hereby amended to read as
11 follows: 72-1163. (a) (1) Each year the board of education of a school
12 district shall conduct an assessment of the educational needs of each
13 attendance center in the district. Such assessment shall include input from
14 board members, teachers employed by the school district, school site
15 councils and attendance center administrators. The complete assessment
16 shall be published on the school district's website.
17 (2) Information obtained from such needs assessment shall be used
18 by the board when approving the budget of the school district to ensure
19 improvement in student academic performance.
20 (3) In the minutes of the meeting at which the board approves its
21 annual budget, the board shall include that such needs assessment was
22 provided to conducted by the board, that the board evaluated such
23 assessment and how the board used such assessment in the approval of the
24 school district's budget.
25 (b) (1) Each year, each member of the board shall receive state
26 assessment results for the school district. Such results shall indicate the
27 number of students who scored at each of the levels 1, 2, 3 and 4 on such
28 assessments.
29 (2) Each year, the board of education of a school district shall review
30 state assessment results and, as part of such review, shall document the
31 following:
32 (A) The barriers that must be overcome to have all students achieve
33 proficiency above level 2 for grade level academic expectations on such
34 assessments;
35 (B) any budget actions, including, but not limited to,
36 recommendations on reallocation of resources that should be taken to
SB 49 2
1 address and remove such barriers; and
2 (C) the amount of time the board estimates it will take for all students
3 to achieve proficiency above level 2 for grade level academic expectations
4 on the state assessments if such budget actions are implemented.
5 (3)(c) The budget of the school district shall allocate or reallocate
6 sufficient moneys in a manner reasonably calculated such that all students
7 may achieve the goal set forth in K.S.A. 72-3218(c), and amendments
8 thereto. The board also shall prepare a summary of the budget for the
9 school district. Any time moneys are allocated to a fund or existing moneys
10 are reallocated from one fund to another fund, such allocation or
11 reallocation shall be identified in the budget and summary. The budgets
12 and summary shall be in the form prescribed by the director pursuant to
13 K.S.A. 79-2926, and amendments thereto.
14 (b)(d) The budgets, the summary of the proposed budget, the needs
15 assessment and the state assessment documentation shall be on file at the
16 administrative offices of the school district and available on the school
17 district's website.
18 (c)(e) The notice required to be published by K.S.A. 79-2929, and
19 amendments thereto, shall include a statement that the budgets, the
20 summary of the proposed budget, the needs assessment and state
21 assessment documentation is on file at the administrative offices of the
22 district and available on the school district's website.
23 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 72-1163 is hereby repealed.
24 Sec. 3. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
25 publication in the statute book.
