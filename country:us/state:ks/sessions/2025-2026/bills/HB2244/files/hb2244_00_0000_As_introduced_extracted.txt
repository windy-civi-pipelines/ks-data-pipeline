Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Insurance
Requested by Representative Sutton
2-4
1 AN ACT concerning the board of pharmacy; prohibiting the board of
2 pharmacy from adopting rules and regulations that would limit or
3 condition the use of telepharmacy; modifying the composition of board
4 members of the board of pharmacy; amending K.S.A. 74-1603 and 74-
5 1604 and K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 65-16,130 and repealing the existing
6 sections.
7
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
9 Section 1. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 65-16,130 is hereby amended to read as
10 follows: 65-16,130. (a) As used in this section:
11 (1) "Telepharmacy" means the practice of pharmacy by a pharmacist
12 located in Kansas using telecommunications or other automations and
13 technologies to deliver personalized, electronically documented, real-time
14 pharmaceutical care to patients or their agents, who are located at sites
15 other than where the pharmacist is located, including prescription
16 dispensing and counseling and to oversee and supervise telepharmacy
17 outlet operations.
18 (2) "Telepharmacy outlet" means a pharmacy site located in Kansas
19 that:
20 (A) Is registered as a pharmacy under the act;
21 (B) is owned by the managing pharmacy;
22 (C) is connected via computer link, video link and audio link or other
23 functionally equivalent telecommunications equipment with a supervising
24 pharmacy located in Kansas; and
25 (D) has a pharmacy technician on site who performs activities under
26 the electronic supervision of a pharmacist located in Kansas.
27 (b) A pharmacist shall be in attendance at the telepharmacy outlet by
28 connecting to the telepharmacy outlet via computer link, video link and
29 audio link or other functionally equivalent telecommunications equipment
30 and shall be available to consult with and assist the pharmacy technician in
31 performing activities.
32 (c) Not later than January 1, 2023, The board shall adopt rules and
33 regulations necessary to specify additional criteria for a managing
34 pharmacy and telepharmacy outlet under this section, including, but not
35 limited to addressing the following:
HB 2244 2
1 (1) Application requirements;
2 (2) structural, security, technology and equipment requirements;
3 (3) staffing, training and electronic supervision requirements;
4 (4) inventory record keeping and storage requirements;
5 (5) labeling requirements;
6 (6) establishment of policies and procedures;
7 (7) the number of telepharmacy outlets that may be operated by a
8 supervising pharmacy;
9 (8) use of automated dispensing machines; and
10 (9) criteria for requesting exemptions or waivers from the
11 requirements set forth in rules and regulations adopted under this
12 subsection.
13 (d) The board shall not limit or condition the use of telepharmacy
14 based on population, distance from a physical pharmacy or network
15 adequacy.
16 (d)(e) This section shall be a part of and supplemental to the
17 pharmacy act of the state of Kansas.
18 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 74-1603 is hereby amended to read as follows: 74-
19 1603. (a) There is hereby created a state board of pharmacy which shall
20 consist of seven members, six of whom shall be licensed pharmacists, and
21 one of whom shall be a representative of the general public.
22 (b) (1) The terms of the board members appointed and serving on the
23 state board of pharmacy on July 1, 2025, shall expire on December 31,
24 2025.
25 (2) A new state board of pharmacy shall be appointed to serve on and
26 after January 1, 2026, and shall have the same powers, duties and
27 functions as its predecessor. Such board shall consist of seven members,
28 who shall be reflect the general types of pharmacies in Kansas, and be
29 appointed as follows:
30 (A) Three pharmacist members appointed by the Kansas pharmacist's
31 association;
32 (B) two pharmacist members employed by chain drug stores or
33 retailers appointed by the commissioner of insurance;
34 (C) one pharmacist member employed by an insurer, specialty or
35 mail order pharmacy serving on behalf of the state employee health plan
36 appointed by the state employee health care commission; and
37 (D) one public non-pharmacist prescription drug purchaser member
38 appointed by the speaker of the house of representatives and the president
39 of the senate. The speaker of the house and the president of the senate
40 shall alternate in appointing such board member.
41 (3) Not later than December 1, 2025, the executive director of the
42 state board of pharmacy shall be notified of all newly appointed board
43 members. Following such initial appointment of board members,
HB 2244 3
1 vacancies occurring on the board shall be filled pursuant to subsection
2 (c).
3 (b)(c) Vacancies occurring on the board other than by expiration of
4 term shall be filled for the unexpired term in the same manner as the
5 original appointment was made. No person who has been appointed to and
6 qualified for two terms as a member of the board of pharmacy shall be
7 eligible to be appointed as a member of the board. On July 1, 2009, the
8 term of office of each existing board member shall be extended by one
9 year.
10 (c) The office of the state board of pharmacy shall be located in the
11 city of Topeka, Kansas.
12 Sec. 3. K.S.A. 74-1604 is hereby amended to read as follows: 74-
13 1604. The governor shall appoint the (a) Members of the board shall be
14 appointed pursuant to K.S.A. 74-1603, and amendments thereto, and such
15 members appointed on and after July 1, 2009 January 1, 2026, shall serve
16 for terms of four years and until their successors are appointed and
17 qualified.
18 (b) No pharmacist shall be eligible for appointment as a member of
19 the board unless such pharmacist has been a resident of the state and
20 actively employed in or engaged in the practice of pharmacy in Kansas for
21 at least five years immediately preceding the date of appointment.
22 Sec. 4. K.S.A. 74-1603 and 74-1604 and K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 65-
23 16,130 are hereby repealed.
24 Sec. 5. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
25 publication in the statute book.
