Section 1:
As Amended by House Committee
Session of 2025
By Committee on Education
Requested by Lt. Col. Keith Marshall on behalf of the Adjutant General's Office
1-31
1 AN ACT concerning education; relating to the national guard; permitting
2 guard members to transfer unused tuition assistance benefits from the
3 Kansas national guard educational assistance act to an eligible
4 dependent; including doctoral and professional degrees in the Kansas
5 national guard educational master's for enhanced readiness and global
6 excellence (EMERGE) program; amending K.S.A. 74-32,145, 74-
7 32,146, 74-32,147 and 74-32,148 and K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,149,
8 74-32,305, 74-32,306, 74-32,308 and 74-32,309 and repealing the
9 existing sections.
10
11 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
12 Section 1. K.S.A. 74-32,145 is hereby amended to read as follows:
13 74-32,145. K.S.A. 74-32,145 through 74-32,149, and amendments thereto,
14 shall be known and may be cited as the Kansas national guard educational
15 assistance act. It is the purpose of the Kansas national guard educational
16 assistance act to establish an educational assistance program under which
17 payment of the tuition and fees charged to eligible members of the Kansas
18 national guard or an eligible dependent of such guard members for
19 enrollment at Kansas educational institutions shall be provided for by the
20 state.
21 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 74-32,146 is hereby amended to read as follows: 74-
22 32,146. As used in the Kansas national guard educational assistance act:
23 (a) "Kansas educational institution" means and includes community
24 colleges, the municipal university, state educational institutions, technical
25 colleges, the institute of technology at Washburn university and accredited
26 independent institutions any not-for-profit independent institution of
27 higher education that is accredited by an institutional accrediting
28 agency recognized by the United States department of education, is
29 operated independently and not controlled or administered by the
30 state or any agency or subdivision thereof, maintains open enrollment,
31 offers online education and offers, exclusively, competency-based
32 education programs.
33 (b) "Eligible guard member" means any current member of the
34 Kansas national guard who is enrolled at a Kansas educational institution
35 and who is not under a suspension of favorable action flag or currently on
HB 2185—Am. by HC 2
1 the unit unfavorable information file. The term eligible guard member
2 does not include within its meaning any member of the Kansas national
3 guard who is the holder of a baccalaureate or higher academic degree, or
4 who does not hold a high school diploma or general educational
5 development (GED) credentials.
6 (c) "Kansas national guard educational assistance program" or
7 "program" means the program established pursuant to the provisions of the
8 Kansas national guard educational assistance act.
9 (d) "Educational program" means a program which that is offered and
10 maintained by a Kansas educational institution and leads to the award of a
11 certificate, diploma or degree upon satisfactory completion of course work
12 requirements.
13 (e) "Dependent" means an individual who is registered as an eligible
14 dependent of the sponsoring eligible guard member in the defense
15 enrollment eligibility reporting system (DEERS).
16 Sec. 3. K.S.A. 74-32,147 is hereby amended to read as follows: 74-
17 32,147. The state board of regents shall may adopt rules and regulations
18 for the administration of the Kansas national guard educational assistance
19 act and shall:
20 (a) Establish a mechanism to ensure distribution of funds for tuition
21 and fee reimbursement to Kansas educational institutions;
22 (b) enter into a cooperative relationship with the adjutant general to
23 ensure efficient operation of the program;
24 (c) develop and effectuate a system of accountability for all
25 disbursements under the program and provide written reports as
26 prescribed; and
27 (d) coordinate with the adjutant general a procedure to ensure initial
28 and on-going ongoing eligibility of all guard members and dependents
29 who are program participants.
30 Sec. 4. K.S.A. 74-32,148 is hereby amended to read as follows: 74-
31 32,148. (a) Subject to the availability of appropriations for the Kansas
32 national guard educational assistance program and within the limits of any
33 such appropriations, every eligible guard member or dependent who is
34 enrolled at a Kansas educational institution and who is participating in the
35 program shall receive assistance each semester in an amount equal to the
36 tuition and required fees for not more than 15 credit hours. The aggregate
37 number of credit hours for which assistance may be provided under the
38 program shall not exceed 150% of the total credit hours required for the
39 eligible guard member or dependent to complete such member's or such
40 dependent's educational program.
41 (b) Eligible guard members may either personally participate or
42 sponsor a dependent to participate in the Kansas national guard
43 educational assistance program. Assistance available to such dependent
HB 2185—Am. by HC 3
1 under the program is subject to the availability of funds after educational
2 benefits are fully funded for all eligible guard members participating in
3 the Kansas national guard educational assistance program.
4 (b)(c) Notwithstanding the provisions of subsection (a), eligible guard
5 members shall not be paid the Kansas national guard educational
6 assistance program shall not pay for the amount of tuition and required
7 fees charged for any course repeated or taken in excess of the requirements
8 for completion of the educational program in which the eligible guard
9 member or dependent is enrolled. The amount of tuition and required fees
10 paid an eligible guard member or dependent pursuant to subsection (a)
11 shall be at a rate not to exceed the maximum rate that would be charged by
12 a state educational institution, as defined in K.S.A. 76-711, and
13 amendments thereto, for enrollment of the eligible guard member or
14 dependent.
15 (c)(d) Amounts of assistance for which an eligible guard member or
16 dependent is eligible to receive under this act shall be offset by the
17 aggregate amount of federal financial assistance received by such guard
18 member or dependent, as a result of active national guard membership, to
19 pay the costs of tuition and fees for enrollment at Kansas educational
20 institutions.
21 Sec. 5. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,149 is hereby amended to read as
22 follows: 74-32,149. (a) (1) In order for a member of the national guard or
23 a dependent to qualify for participation in the Kansas national guard
24 educational assistance program, an eligible guard member must shall
25 agree, in writing, to complete such member's current service obligation in
26 the Kansas national guard and serve actively in good standing with the
27 Kansas national guard for not less than 24 months upon completion of the
28 last semester for which the member or dependent receives assistance under
29 the program.
30 (2) In order for a member of the national guard to qualify for
31 assistance from the Kansas national guard educational assistance
32 program, an eligible guard member shall:
33 (A) Hold a high school diploma or high school equivalency
34 credential;
35 (B) be enrolled at a Kansas educational institution; and
36 (C) not hold a baccalaureate or higher academic degree.
37 (3) An eligible guard member may only sponsor one dependent to
38 participate in the Kansas national guard educational program during such
39 member's service.
40 (4) To qualify for assistance from the Kansas national guard
41 educational assistance program, a dependent shall:
42 (A) Hold a high school diploma or high school equivalency
43 credential;
HB 2185—Am. by HC 4
1 (B) be enrolled at a Kansas educational institution;
2 (C) not hold a baccalaureate or higher academic degree; and
3 (D) complete and submit the Kansas student aid application with the
4 state board of regents.
5 (2)(5) Prior to becoming eligible for participation in the program,
6 each eligible guard member or dependent shall submit the free application
7 for federal student aid, and apply for any other federal tuition assistance
8 that such member or dependent also may be eligible to receive.
9 (b) In order to remain eligible for participation in the program, an
10 eligible guard member must or dependent shall remain in good standing at
11 the Kansas educational institution where enrolled, make satisfactory
12 progress toward completion of the requirements of the educational
13 program in which enrolled, and maintain a grade point average of not less
14 than 2.0 and. The eligible guard member shall maintain satisfactory
15 participation in the Kansas national guard. It shall be the responsibility of
16 the eligible guard member to obtain a certificate from the member's
17 commanding officer attesting to the member's satisfactory participation in
18 the Kansas national guard and to present the certificate to the educational
19 institution, in order to obtain a payment under this act. The certificate shall
20 be presented at the time payment is requested for completed courses. Upon
21 completion of each semester, each eligible guard member or dependent
22 receiving assistance under the program shall submit a transcript of the
23 credit hours earned by such guard member or dependent, including the
24 grades for credit hours, to such eligible member's unit of assignment.
25 (c) Upon failure of any person, who as an eligible guard member
26 received payments or whose dependent received payments under the
27 Kansas national guard educational assistance act, to satisfy the agreement
28 to continue service in the Kansas national guard as provided by subsection
29 (a)(1), such person shall pay to the state of Kansas an amount to be
30 determined as follows:
31 (1) Determine the total amount of assistance paid to such member or
32 dependent under the program;
33 (2) divide the amount determined under subsection (c)(1) by 24; and
34 (3) multiply the amount determined under subsection (c)(2) by the
35 number of months such member did not serve as required by subsection
36 (a)(1). The resulting product is the total amount of recoupment from such
37 member.
38 All amounts paid to the state under this subsection shall be deposited in
39 the state treasury and credited to the Kansas national guard educational
40 assistance program repayment fund created by K.S.A. 74-32,150, and
41 amendments thereto.
42 (d) Any eligible guard member that received payments, or whose
43 dependent received payments, under the program but has failed to satisfy
HB 2185—Am. by HC 5
1 the agreement to continue service in the Kansas national guard as provided
2 by subsection (a)(1) by reason of extenuating circumstances or extreme
3 hardship may request a waiver from recoupment. Such request shall be in
4 writing and submitted through such member's chain of command to the
5 Kansas national guard education services office. The chief of staff of the
6 Kansas army national guard or the director of staff for the Kansas air
7 national guard shall review all requests for a waiver from recoupment, and
8 the decision to issue such waiver shall be made by either officer as such
9 officer deems appropriate.
10 Sec. 6. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,305 is hereby amended to read as
11 follows: 74-32,305. (a) K.S.A. 74-32,305 through 74-32,310, and
12 amendments thereto, shall be known and may be cited as the Kansas
13 national guard educational master's for enhanced readiness and global
14 excellence (EMERGE) program.
15 (b) The purpose of the Kansas national guard EMERGE program is to
16 establish a master's an advanced degree assistance program under which
17 payment of the tuition and fees charged to eligible members of the Kansas
18 national guard for enrollment in master's advanced degree programs at
19 Kansas educational institutions shall be provided by the state pursuant to
20 the EMERGE program.
21 Sec. 7. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,306 is hereby amended to read as
22 follows: 74-32,306. As used in the Kansas national guard educational
23 master's for enhanced readiness and global excellence (EMERGE)
24 program:
25 (a) "Educational programAdvanced degree" means a master's degree
26 program offered or maintained by a Kansas educational institution that
27 leads to the award of a master's, professional degree or doctorate awarded
28 to an eligible guard member upon satisfactory completion of the course
29 work requirements of such degree program offered or maintained by a
30 Kansas educational institution.
31 (b) "Doctorate" means a degree requiring three or more academic
32 years of full-time academic study or the equivalent in part-time attendance
33 that follows the successful completion of a baccalaureate or master's
34 degree. A "doctorate" may be either a research or a professional practice
35 degree.
36 (c) "Eligible guard member" means any member of the Kansas
37 national guard who has been accepted into an eligible master's advanced
38 degree program and who is not under a suspension of favorable flag action
39 or on the unit unfavorable information file. "Eligible guard member"
40 includes nonconcurrent guard members accepted into an eligible
41 professional degree program if they are qualified to join the Kansas
42 national guard.
43 (c)(d) "Kansas educational institution" means a state educational

1 institution, as defined in K.S.A. 76-711, and amendments thereto,
2 Washburn university or an accredited independent institution, as defined in
3 K.S.A. 72-3222, and amendments thereto.
4 (d)(e) "Kansas national guard educational master's for enhanced
5 readiness and global excellence program" or "EMERGE program" means
6 the program established pursuant to the provisions of the Kansas national
7 guard educational master's for enhanced readiness and global excellence
8 program.
9 (f) "Master's degree" means a degree requiring not less than one year
10 of academic work or the equivalent in part-time attendance and follows
11 the successful completion of a baccalaureate degree.
12 (g) "Professional degree" means a degree that requires not less than
13 three years of full-time study or the equivalent in part-time attendance that
14 follows the successful completion of a baccalaureate degree, such as a
15 juris doctor degree or a physician's assistant degree.
16 Sec. 8. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,308 is hereby amended to read as
17 follows: 74-32,308. (a) Subject to the availability of appropriations for the
18 Kansas national guard EMERGE program and within the limits of any
19 such appropriations, except as provided in subsections (b) and (c), every
20 eligible national guard member who is enrolled at a Kansas educational
21 institution and participating in the program shall receive assistance each
22 semester in an amount equal to the tuition and required fees for not more
23 than 15 hours. The aggregate number of credit hours for which assistance
24 may be provided under the program shall not exceed 150% of the total
25 credit hours required for the eligible guard member to complete such
26 member's master's advanced degree program.
27 (b) Notwithstanding the provisions of subsection (a), eligible guard
28 members shall not be paid the EMERGE program shall not pay for the
29 amount of tuition and fees charged for any course repeated or taken in
30 excess of the requirements for completion of the master's advanced degree
31 program in which the eligible guard member is enrolled. The amount of
32 tuition and required fees paid an eligible guard member pursuant to
33 subsection (a) shall be at a rate not to exceed the maximum rate that would
34 be charged by a state educational institution for enrollment of the eligible
35 guard member.
36 (c) Amounts of assistance for which an eligible guard member is
37 eligible to receive under this act shall be offset by the aggregate amount of
38 federal or institutional tuition assistance received by such eligible guard
39 member, as a result of active national guard membership, to pay costs of
40 tuition and fees for enrollment at Kansas educational institutions.
41 Sec. 9. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,309 is hereby amended to read as
42 follows: 74-32,309. (a) On or before July 1 of each academic year, The
43 adjutant general shall select up to 100 eligible national guard members

1 annually who applied to the program to receive assistance from such
2 program. The number of eligible guard members in the program shall not
3 exceed 200 eligible guard members in any one school year.
4 (b) (1) In order to qualify for participation in the Kansas national
5 guard EMERGE program, an eligible national guard member shall agree,
6 in writing, to complete such member's current service obligation in the
7 Kansas national guard and serve actively in good standing with the Kansas
8 national guard or in a duty status affiliated with the Kansas national guard
9 for not less than 48 months upon completion of the last semester for which
10 the member receives assistance under the program for a master's degree
11 and for not less than 72 months upon graduation for a doctorate or
12 professional degree for which the member receives assistance under the
13 program.
14 (2) Prior to becoming eligible for participation in the program, each
15 eligible guard member shall submit the free application for federal student
16 aid and apply for any other federal tuition assistance that such member
17 also may be eligible to receive.
18 (c) In order to remain eligible for participation in the program, an
19 eligible guard member shall remain in good standing at the Kansas
20 educational institution where such member is enrolled, make satisfactory
21 progress toward completion of the requirements of such member's master's
22 advanced degree program, maintain a grade point average of not less than
23 2.75 for a master's degree or a 3.0 for a doctorate or professional degree
24 and maintain satisfactory participation in the Kansas national guard.
25 (d) (1) Upon failure of any eligible guard member who received
26 payments under the Kansas national guard EMERGE program to satisfy
27 the agreement to continue service in the Kansas national guard as provided
28 by subsection (a), such person shall pay to the state of Kansas an amount
29 to be determined as follows:
30 (A) Determine the total amount of assistance paid to such member
31 under the program;
32 (B) divide the amount determined under subsection (d)(1)(A) by 48
33 for a master's degree or 72 for a doctorate or professional degree; and
34 (C) multiply the amount determined under subsection (d)(1)(B) by
35 the number of months such member did not serve as required by
36 subsection (a). The resulting product is the total amount of recoupment to
37 be paid by such member.
38 (2) All amounts paid to the state under this subsection shall be
39 deposited in the state treasury and credited to the Kansas national guard
40 EMERGE program repayment fund created by section 6 K.S.A. 2024
41 Supp. 74-32,310, and amendments thereto.
42 (e) Any eligible guard member that received payments under the
43 program but has failed to satisfy the agreement to continue service in the

6 army national guard or the director of staff for the Kansas air national
7 guard shall review all requests for a waiver from recoupment, and the
8 decision to issue such waiver shall be made by either officer as such
9 officer deems appropriate.
