Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Local Government
Requested by Whitney Damron on behalf of the City of Topeka
1-27
1 AN ACT concerning private rental housing; requiring landlords of
2 government-subsidized housing to submit to code inspection by cities
3 or counties; amending K.S.A. 12-16,138 and repealing the existing
4 section.
5
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
7 Section 1. K.S.A. 12-16,138 is hereby amended to read as follows:
8 12-16,138. (a) Subject to subsection (c), no city or county shall adopt,
9 enforce or maintain a residential property licensing ordinance or resolution
10 which that includes a requirement for periodic interior inspections of
11 privately owned residential property for city or county code violations
12 unless the lawful occupant has consented to such interior inspections. This
13 subsection shall not apply to inspections of mixed-use residential and
14 commercial property. This subsection shall not prohibit a city or county
15 from conducting plan reviews, periodic construction inspections or final
16 occupancy inspections as required by building permits.
17 (b) Any lawful occupant residing in privately owned residential
18 housing located within the corporate limits of a city may request an
19 inspection at any time by the city or, if the property is located in the
20 unincorporated area of the county, by the county to determine code
21 violations.
22 (c) A city or county may require periodic property inspections of
23 privately owned residential housing property when the owner of such
24 property is receiving governmental rental subsidies. The city or county
25 shall provide reasonable notice to the tenants of the date and time of the
26 inspection. The landlord may be required to perform random inspections
27 at the request of the city or county in response to code violation
28 complaints. If a tenant objects to an inspection, a city or county shall
29 obtain an administrative search to facilitate the inspection.
30 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 12-16,138 is hereby repealed.
31 Sec. 3. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
32 publication in the statute book.
