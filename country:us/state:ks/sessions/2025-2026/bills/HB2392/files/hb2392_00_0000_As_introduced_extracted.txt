Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Appropriations
Requested by Representative Wasinger
2-26
1 AN ACT concerning nursing workforce development; relating to nursing
2 schools; setting maximum education levels for instructors at nursing
3 schools as a requirement for state approval; amending K.S.A. 65-1119
4 and repealing the existing section.
5
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
7 Section 1. K.S.A. 65-1119 is hereby amended to read as follows: 65-
8 1119. (a) Application for approval. An approved school of nursing is one
9 which that has been approved as such by the board as meeting the
10 standards of this act, and the rules and regulations of the board. An
11 institution desiring to conduct an approved school of professional or
12 practical nursing shall apply to the board for approval and submit
13 satisfactory proof that it such school is prepared to and will maintain the
14 standards and basic professional nursing curriculum or the required
15 curriculum for practical nursing, as the case may be, as prescribed by this
16 act and by the rules and regulations of the board. Applications shall be
17 made in writing on forms supplied by the board and shall be submitted to
18 the board together with the application fee fixed by the board. The
19 approval of a school of nursing shall not exceed 10 years after the granting
20 of such approval by the board. An institution desiring to continue to
21 conduct an approved school of professional or practical nursing shall apply
22 to the board for the renewal of approval and submit satisfactory proof that
23 it such school will maintain the standards and basic professional nursing
24 curriculum or the required curriculum for practical nursing, as the case
25 may be, as prescribed by this act and by the rules and regulations of the
26 board. Applications for renewal of approval shall be made in writing on
27 forms supplied by the board. Each school of nursing shall submit annually
28 to the board an annual fee fixed by the board by rules and regulations to
29 maintain the approval status.
30 (b) Schools for professional nurses. To qualify as an approved school
31 for professional nurses, the school must shall be conducted in the state of
32 Kansas, and shall apply to the board and submit evidence that: (1) It Such
33 school is prepared to carry out the professional curriculum as prescribed in
34 the rules and regulations of the board; (2) such school's faculty possess a
35 nursing or directly related medical degree awarded by a state or
HB 2392 2
1 nationally accredited school of nursing or medical school approved by the
2 board that is at least one level more advanced than the degree awarded by
3 the program in which they are teaching. Additional or more advanced
4 credentials for faculty shall not be required; and (2) it (3) such school is
5 prepared to meet such other standards as shall be established by this law
6 and the rules and regulations of the board.
7 (c) Schools for practical nurses. To qualify as an approved school for
8 practical nurses, the school must shall be conducted in the state of Kansas,
9 and shall apply to the board and submit evidence that: (1) It Such school is
10 prepared to carry out the curriculum as prescribed in the rules and
11 regulations of the board; (2) such school's faculty possess a nursing or
12 directly related medical degree awarded by a state or nationally
13 accredited school of nursing or medical school approved by the board that
14 is at least one level more advanced than the degree awarded by the
15 program in which they are teaching. Additional or more advanced
16 credentials for faculty shall not be required; and (2) it (3) such school is
17 prepared to meet such other standards as shall be established by this law
18 and the rules and regulations of the board.
19 (d) Survey. The board shall prepare and maintain a list of approved
20 schools for both professional and practical nurses whose graduates, if they
21 have the other necessary qualifications provided in this act, shall be
22 eligible to apply for a license as a registered professional nurse or as a
23 licensed practical nurse. A survey of the institution or institutions and of
24 the schools applying for approval shall be made by an authorized
25 employee of the board or members of the board, who shall submit a
26 written report of the survey to the board. If, in the opinion of the board, the
27 requirements as prescribed by the board in its rules and regulations for an
28 approved school for professional nurses or for practical nurses are met, it
29 the board shall so approve the school as either a school for professional
30 nurses or practical nurses, as the case may be. The board shall resurvey
31 approved schools on a periodic basis as determined by rules and
32 regulations. If the board determines that any approved school of nursing is
33 not maintaining the standards required by this act and by rules and
34 regulations prescribed by the board, notice thereof in writing, specifying
35 the failures of such school, shall be given immediately to the school. A
36 school which that fails to correct such conditions to the satisfaction of the
37 board within a reasonable time shall be removed from the list of approved
38 schools of nursing until such time as the school shall comply complies
39 with the standards. All approved schools shall maintain accurate and
40 current records showing in full the theoretical and practical courses given
41 to each student.
42 (e) Providers of continuing nursing education. (1) To qualify as an
43 approved provider of continuing nursing education offerings, persons,
HB 2392 3
1 organizations or institutions proposing to provide such continuing nursing
2 education offerings shall apply to the board for approval and submit
3 evidence that the applicant is prepared to meet the standards and
4 requirements established by the rules and regulations of the board for such
5 continuing nursing education offerings. Initial applications shall be made
6 in writing on forms supplied by the board and shall be submitted to the
7 board together with the application fee fixed by the board.
8 (2) AAn "approved long-term provider" means a person, organization
9 or institution that is responsible for the development, administration and
10 evaluation of continuing nursing education programs and offerings and
11 that has been approved as qualified by the board. Qualification as a long-
12 term approved provider of continuing nursing education offerings shall
13 expire five years after the granting of such approval by the board. An
14 approved long-term provider of continuing nursing education offerings
15 shall submit annually to the board the annual fee established by rules and
16 regulations, along with an annual report for the previous fiscal year.
17 Applications for renewal as an approved long-term provider of continuing
18 nursing education offerings shall be made in writing on forms supplied by
19 the board.
20 (3) Qualification as an approved provider of a single continuing
21 nursing education offering, which may be offered once or multiple times,
22 shall expire two years after the granting of such approval by the board.
23 Approved single continuing nursing education providers shall not be
24 subject to an annual fee or annual report.
25 (4) In accordance with rules and regulations adopted by the board, the
26 board may approve individual educational offerings for continuing nursing
27 education, which shall not be subject to approval under other subsections
28 of this section.
29 (5) The board shall accept offerings as approved continuing nursing
30 education presented by: colleges that are approved by a state or the
31 national department of education and providers approved by other state
32 boards of nursing, the national league for nursing, the national federation
33 of licensed practical nurses, the American nurses credentialing center or
34 other such such other national organizations as listed in rules and
35 regulations adopted by the board.
36 (6) An individual designated by a provider of continuing nursing
37 education offerings as an individual responsible for CNE who has held this
38 position for the provider at least five years immediately prior to January 1,
39 1997, shall not be required to have a baccalaureate or higher academic
40 degree in order to be designated by such provider as the individual
41 responsible for CNE.
42 (f) Criteria for evaluating out-of-state schools. For the purpose of
43 determining whether an applicant for licensure who is a graduate of a
HB 2392 4
1 school of professional or practical nursing located outside this state meets
2 the requirements of item (2) of subsection (a) of K.S.A. 65-1115(a)(2), and
3 amendments thereto, or the requirements of item (2) of subsection (a) of
4 K.S.A. 65-1116(a)(2), and amendments thereto, as appropriate, the board
5 by rules and regulations shall establish criteria for determining whether a
6 particular school of professional nursing located outside this state
7 maintains standards which that are at least equal to schools of professional
8 nursing which that are approved by the board and whether a particular
9 school of practical nursing located outside this state maintains standards
10 which that are at least equal to schools of practical nursing which that are
11 approved by the board. The board may send a questionnaire developed by
12 the board to any school of professional or practical nursing located outside
13 this state for which the board does not have sufficient information to
14 determine whether the school meets the standards established under this
15 subsection (f). The questionnaire providing the necessary information shall
16 be completed and returned to the board in order for the school to be
17 considered for approval. The board may contract with investigative
18 agencies, commissions or consultants to assist the board in obtaining
19 information about schools. In entering such contracts the authority to
20 approve schools shall remain solely with the board.
21 (g) The board may accept nationally accredited schools of nursing as
22 defined in rule and regulation.
23 (1) Schools of nursing which that have received accreditation from a
24 board recognized national nursing accreditation agency shall file evidence
25 of initial accreditation with the board and shall file all reports from the
26 accrediting agency and any notice of any change in school accreditation
27 status. The board may grant approval based upon evidence of such
28 accreditation.
29 (2) Schools of nursing holding approval based upon national
30 accreditation are also responsible for complying shall also comply with all
31 other requirements as determined by rules and regulations of the board.
32 (3) The board may grant approval to a school of nursing with national
33 accreditation for a continuing period not to exceed 10 years.
34 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 65-1119 is hereby repealed.
35 Sec. 3. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
36 publication in the statute book.
