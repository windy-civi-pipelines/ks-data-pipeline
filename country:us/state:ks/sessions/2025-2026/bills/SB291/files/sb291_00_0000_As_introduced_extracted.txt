1 AN ACT concerning health professions and practices; relating to
2 optometry; modifying certain provisions of the optometry law related to
3 scope of practice, definitions and credentialing requirements; amending
4 K.S.A. 65-1501, 65-1509a, 65-1512, 65-1523 and 74-1504 and K.S.A.
5 2024 Supp. 65-1501a and 65-1505 and repealing the existing sections;
6 also repealing K.S.A. 65-1514 and 74-1505.
7
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
9 Section 1. K.S.A. 65-1501 is hereby amended to read as follows: 65-
10 1501. (a) The practice of optometry means:
11 (1) The examination of the human eye and its adnexae adnexa and
12 the employment of objective or subjective means or methods, (including
13 the administering, prescribing or dispensing, of topical pharmaceutical
14 drugs), for the purpose of diagnosing the refractive, muscular, or
15 pathological condition thereof;
16 (2) the use of medical devices and the prescribing, dispensing or
17 adapting of lenses, (including any ophthalmic lenses which that are
18 classified as drugs by any law of the United States or of this state), prisms,
19 low vision rehabilitation services, orthoptic exercises and visual training
20 therapy for the relief of any insufficiencies or abnormal conditions of the
21 human eye and its adnexae adnexa; and
22 (3) the use of medical devices and the prescribing, administering or
23 dispensing of topical pharmaceutical drugs and oral drugs through all
24 routes of administration, except intraocular injections, for the
25 examination, diagnosis and treatment of ocular conditions and any
26 insufficiencies or abnormal conditions of the human eye and its adnexae
27 including adult open-angle glaucoma adnexa;
28 (4) the removal of non‐perforating foreign bodies from the cornea,
29 conjunctiva or eyelids, removal of eyelashes, scraping the cornea for
30 diagnostic tests, smears or cultures, the dilation, probing, irrigation or
31 closing the tear drainage structure of the eye, expression of conjunctival
32 follicles or cysts, debridement of the corneal epithelium, incision and
33 curettage of a chalazion, removal and biopsy of skin lesions without
34 known malignancy, laser capsulotomy, laser trabeculoplasty and laser
35 peripheral iridotomy; and
36 (5) the performance of additional procedures that are not otherwise
SB 291 2
1 prohibited by subsection (b) that are within the scope of a licensee's
2 education and training for the treatment of any insufficiencies or
3 abnormal conditions of the human eye and its adnexa as authorized
4 pursuant to rules and regulations adopted by the board.
5 (b) The practice of optometry shall not include the performance of
6 the following procedures: (1) The management and treatment of glaucoma,
7 except as provided in subsection (a); (2) the performance of surgery,
8 including the use of lasers for surgical purposes, except that licensees may
9 remove non-perforating foreign bodies from the cornea, conjunctiva or
10 eyelids; remove eyelashes; scrape the cornea for diagnostic tests, smears or
11 cultures; dilate, probe, irrigate or close by punctal plug the tear drainage
12 structures of the eye; express conjunctival follicles or cysts; debridement
13 of the corneal epithelium and co-management of post-operative care; or
14 (3) the performance of procedures requiring anesthesia administered by
15 injection or general anesthesia Retinal surgery, penetrating keratoplasty or
16 corneal transplant, administration of or surgery performed under general
17 anesthesia, surgery related to the removal of the eye from a living human
18 being, surgical extraction of the crystalline lens, surgical intraocular
19 implants, incisional or excisional surgery of the extraocular muscles,
20 surgery of the bony orbit, laser‐assisted in situ keratomileusis or
21 photorefractive keratectomy, YAG laser vitreolysis, and surgery of the
22 eyelid for cosmetic or mechanical repair of blepharochalasis, ptosis and
23 tarsorrhaphy. However, an optometrist may provide pre-operative and
24 post-operative care for the procedures in this subsection.
25 (c) A licensee shall be held to a standard of care in the diagnosis and
26 treatment of adult open-angle glaucoma commensurate to with that of a
27 person licensed to practice medicine and surgery, who exercises that
28 degree of skill and proficiency commonly exercised by an ordinary,
29 skillful, careful and prudent person licensed to practice medicine and
30 surgery.
31 (d) Under the direction and supervision of a licensee, a licensed
32 professional nurse, licensed practical nurse, licensed physical therapist and
33 licensed occupational therapist may assist in the provision of low vision
34 rehabilitation services in addition to such other services which that such
35 licensed professional nurse, licensed practical nurse, licensed physical
36 therapist and licensed occupational therapist is authorized by law to
37 provide under subsection (d) of K.S.A. 65-1113(d), subsection (h) of
38 K.S.A. 65-1124(h), subsection (b) of K.S.A. 65-2901(b) and subsection (b)
39 of K.S.A. 65-5402(b), and amendments thereto.

40 (b) All applicants for licensure, in addition to successfully completing
41 all other requirements for licensure, shall submit evidence satisfactory to
42 the board of professional liability insurance in an amount acceptable to the
43 board.
