Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Federal and State Affairs
Requested by Representative Corbet
1-16
1 AN ACT concerning wildlife; licenses; requiring the Kansas department of
2 wildlife and parks to offer discounted resident senior combination
3 hunting and fishing passes to residents of this state who are 65 years of
4 age or older; removing the expiration date for Kansas kids lifetime
5 combination hunting and fishing licenses; amending K.S.A. 2024 Supp.
6 32-9,101 and repealing the existing section; reviving and amending
7 K.S.A. 32-9,100 and repealing the revived section; also repealing
8 K.S.A. 32-9,100, as amended by section 64 of chapter 7 of the 2023
9 Session Laws of Kansas.
10
11 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
12 Section 1. K.S.A. 32-9,100 is hereby revived and amended to read as
13 follows: 32-9,100. On and after January 1, 2013 July 1, 2020, the Kansas
14 department of wildlife, and parks and tourism shall offer a resident senior
15 combination hunting and fishing pass to residents of this state who are 65
16 years of age or more. The fee for such pass shall be an amount not to
17 exceed 1/ the fee for a general combination lifetime hunting and fishing
8
18 license. The provisions of this section shall expire on June 30, 2020.
19 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 32-9,101 is hereby amended to read as
20 follows: 32-9,101. (a) (1) The secretary of wildlife and parks shall issue
21 and make available a Kansas kids lifetime combination hunting and
22 fishing license to any child who is a resident, as defined in K.S.A. 32-701,
23 and amendments thereto, and:
24 (A) Five years of age or younger, upon payment of a license fee that
25 shall not exceed $300; or
26 (B) six or seven years of age, upon payment of a license fee that shall
27 not exceed $500.
28 (2) Such license fee may be paid on behalf of such child.
29 (b) The secretary of wildlife and parks shall report annually to the
30 house of representatives agriculture and natural resources budget
31 committee and the senate committee on agriculture and natural resources,
32 or the successor of such committees, on the number of such Kansas kids
33 lifetime combination hunting and fishing licenses issued by the Kansas
34 department of wildlife and parks.
35 (c) The provisions of this section shall expire on July 1, 2032.
HB 2028 2
1 Sec. 3. K.S.A. 32-9,100, as revived and amended by section 1 of this
2 act, K.S.A. 32-9,100, as amended by section 64 of chapter 7 of the 2023
3 Session Laws of Kansas, and K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 32-9,101 are hereby
4 repealed.
5 Sec. 4. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
6 publication in the Kansas register.
