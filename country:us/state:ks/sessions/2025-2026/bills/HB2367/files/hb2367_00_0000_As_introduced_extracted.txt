Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Health and Human Services
Requested by Kevin Barone on behalf of the Kansas Naturopathic Doctors
Association
2-7
1 AN ACT concerning health and healthcare; relating to naturopathic
2 doctors; providing naturopathic doctors a certificate of authorization for
3 a business entity to practice medicine; amending K.S.A. 17-2710 and
4 K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 65-28,134 and repealing the existing sections.
5
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
7 Section 1. K.S.A. 17-2710 is hereby amended to read as follows: 17-
8 2710. A professional corporation may be organized only for the purpose of
9 rendering one type of professional service and service ancillary thereto and
10 shall not engage in any other business, except that a single professional
11 corporation may be organized to and render professional services under
12 any two or more of the types set forth in items (2), (6), (13) and (17) of
13 subsection (b) of K.S.A. 17-2707(b)(2), (6), (13) and (17), and
14 amendments thereto;, under any two or more of the types set forth in items
15 (4), (5), (7), (8), (9), (11), (12), (14), (15), (16), (18), (20), (22), (23), (26)
16 or (27) of subsection (b) of K.S.A. 17-2707(b)(4), (5), (7), (8), (9), (11),
17 (12), (14), (15), (16), (18), (20), (22), (23), (26), (27) and (28), and
18 amendments thereto;, under any two or more of the types set forth in items
19 (8), (9), (18), (24), (25), (26) and (27) of subsection (b) of K.S.A. 17-
20 2707(b)(8), (9), (18), (24), (25), (26), (27) and (28), and amendments
21 thereto;, or under the types set forth in items (16) and (25) of subsection
22 (b) of K.S.A. 17-2707(b)(16) and (25), and amendments thereto, but shall
23 be deemed to have the following purposes, whether or not authorized by
24 its article of incorporation:
25 (a) To purchase, receive, lease, or otherwise acquire, own, hold,
26 improve, use and otherwise deal in and with, real or personal property, or
27 any interest therein, wherever situated;
28 (b) to purchase, receive, or otherwise acquire, own, hold, vote, use,
29 employ, sell, mortgage, lend, pledge, or otherwise dispose of, and
30 otherwise use and deal in and with, shares of other interests in, or
31 obligations of, other domestic or foreign corporations, associations,
32 partnerships or individuals, insurance or annuities in any form, or direct or
33 indirect obligations of the United States or of any other government, state,
34 territory, governmental district or municipality or of any instrumentality
HB 2367 2
1 thereof;
2 (c) to pay pensions and establish pension plans, profit-sharing plans,
3 stock bonus plans, stock option plans and other incentive plans for any or
4 all of its directors, officers and employees;
5 (d) to do all things necessary or incidental to the practice of the
6 profession which that the professional corporation is authorized to
7 practice.
8 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 65-28,134 is hereby amended to read as
9 follows: 65-28,134. (a) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, a
10 business entity issued a certificate of authorization by the board may
11 employ or contract with one or more licensees of the board for the purpose
12 of providing professional services for which such licensees hold a valid
13 license issued by the board. Nothing in the Kansas healing arts act shall be
14 construed to prohibit a licensee from being employed by or under contract
15 to provide professional services for a business entity granted a certificate
16 of authorization pursuant to this section. Medical care facilities, as defined
17 by K.S.A. 65-425, and amendments thereto, that are in compliance with
18 department of health and environment licensure requirements are exempt
19 from the provisions of this section. Nothing contained herein shall be
20 construed to allow a corporation to practice optometry or dentistry, except
21 as otherwise provided in K.S.A. 17-2706, and amendments thereto.
22 (b) (1) A business entity may apply to the state board of healing arts
23 for a certificate of authorization, on a form and in a manner prescribed by
24 the state board of healing arts, and shall include the following information:
25 (A) The name of the business entity;
26 (B) a list of the names of the owners and officers of the business
27 entity;
28 (C) a description of the apportionment of liability of all partners or
29 owners, if the business entity is organized as a limited partnership or a
30 limited liability company;
31 (D) a list of each responsible official if the business entity is
32 organized as a governmental unit; and
33 (E) a list of all licensed physicians and, chiropractors and
34 naturopathic doctors to be hired by the business entity.
35 (2) As a condition of certification, a business entity shall be required
36 to provide the state board of healing arts evidence of the following:
37 (A) The address of the business entity;
38 (B) a city or county occupational license; and
39 (C) licensure of all physicians and, chiropractors and naturopathic
40 doctors to be employed by the business entity.
41 (3) A business entity applying for certification shall remit a fee set by
42 the state board of healing arts through rules and regulations, not to exceed
43 $1,000.
HB 2367 3
1 (c) (1) If the state board of healing arts finds that such business entity
2 is in compliance with all of the requirements of this section, the state board
3 of healing arts shall issue a certificate of authorization to such business
4 entity designating the business entity as authorized to employ individuals
5 licensed to practice medicine and surgery or, chiropractic or naturopathy,
6 as applicable.
7 (2) A certificate of authorization shall be renewed annually and
8 accompanied by a fee to be fixed by the state board of healing arts. The
9 renewal fee shall be accompanied by a form prescribed by the state board
10 of healing arts.
11 (d) Except as provided in K.S.A. 40-3403, and amendments thereto,
12 no business entity issued a certificate of authorization under this section
13 shall be relieved of responsibility for the conduct or acts of its agents or
14 employees by reason of its compliance with the provisions of this section,
15 nor shall any individual licensed to practice the healing arts be relieved of
16 responsibility and liability for services performed by reason of
17 employment or relationship with such business entity. Nothing in this
18 section shall exempt any business entity from the provisions of any other
19 law applicable to the business entity.
20 (e) A business entity issued a certificate of authorization under this
21 section shall not:
22 (1) In any manner, directly or indirectly, interfere with, diminish,
23 restrict, substitute its judgment for or otherwise exercise control over the
24 independent professional judgment and decisions of its employed licensees
25 as it relates to the care of patients; or
26 (2) prohibit or restrict any employed licensee from discussing with or
27 disclosing to any patient or other individual any medically appropriate
28 healthcare information that such licensee deems appropriate regarding the
29 nature of treatment options, the risks or alternatives thereto, the process
30 used or the decision made by the business entity to approve or deny
31 healthcare services, the availability of alternate therapies, consultations or
32 tests, or from advocating on behalf of the patient.
33 (f) As used in this section:
34 (1) (A) "Business entity" means an employer located in Kansas that
35 utilizes electronic medical records and offers medicine and surgery or,
36 chiropractic or naturopathy services solely for its employees and the
37 dependents of such employees at the employer's work site;, an
38 organization that is licensed to sell accident and sickness insurance in the
39 state that and is also a mutual or non-profit health carrier that utilizes
40 electronic medical records, or a wholly owned subsidiary of such
41 organization that provides medical services solely for the organization's
42 enrollees and dependents of such enrollees;, or an information technology
43 company that designs, utilizes and provides electronic medical records for
HB 2367 4
1 businesses and worksite medical clinics for employers located in Kansas
2 and offers medicine and surgery or, chiropractic or naturopathy services
3 solely to its employees and the dependents of such employees at the
4 employer's work sites in Kansas.
5 (B) "Business entity" does not include medical care facilities under
6 K.S.A. 65-425, and amendments thereto, corporations licensed under
7 K.S.A. 40-3214, and amendments thereto, and professional corporations
8 organized pursuant to the professional corporation law of Kansas.
9 (2) "Physician" means a person licensed by the state board of healing
10 arts to practice medicine and surgery.
11 (3) "Licensee" means a person licensed by the state board of healing
12 arts to practice medicine and surgery or, chiropractic or naturopathy and
13 whose license is in a full active status and has not been revoked,
14 suspended, limited or placed under probationary conditions.
15 (g) A business entity's certificate of authorization may be revoked,
16 suspended or limited, may be or publicly censured or placed under
17 probationary conditions, or an application for a certificate or for
18 reinstatement of a certificate may be denied upon a finding of the
19 existence of any of the following grounds:
20 (1) The business entity has committed fraud or misrepresentation in
21 applying for or securing an original, renewal or reinstated certificate.
22 (2) The business entity has willfully or repeatedly violated this act,
23 the pharmacy act of the state of Kansas or the uniform controlled
24 substances act or any rules and regulations adopted pursuant thereto, or
25 any rules and regulations of the secretary of health and environment that
26 are relevant to the practice of the healing arts.
27 (3) The business entity has had a certificate, or equivalent
28 authorization, to employ licensees to practice the healing arts revoked,
29 suspended or limited, has been censured or has had other disciplinary
30 action taken or has had an application for a certificate or license denied, by
31 the proper licensing authority of another state.
32 (4) The business entity has violated any lawful rule and regulation
33 promulgated by the board.
34 (5) The business entity has failed to report or reveal the knowledge
35 required to be reported or revealed under K.S.A. 65-28,122, and
36 amendments thereto.
37 (6) The business entity has failed to report to the board any adverse
38 action taken against the business entity by another state or licensing
39 jurisdiction, a governmental agency, by a law enforcement agency or a
40 court for acts or conduct similar to acts or conduct that would constitute
41 grounds for disciplinary action under this section.
42 (7) The business entity has engaged in conduct likely to deceive,
43 defraud or harm the public.
HB 2367 5
1 (8) The business entity has engaged in conduct that violates patient
2 trust and exploits the licensee-patient relationship for corporate gain.
3 (9) The business entity has used any false, fraudulent or deceptive
4 statement in any document connected with the practice of the healing arts,
5 including the intentional falsifying or fraudulent altering of a patient
6 healthcare record.
7 (10) The business entity has failed to furnish to the board, or its
8 investigators or representatives, any information legally requested by the
9 board.
10 (11) The business entity has had, or failed to report to the board, any
11 adverse judgment, award or settlement against the business entity resulting
12 from a medical liability claim related to acts or conduct similar to acts or
13 conduct that would constitute grounds for disciplinary action under this
14 section.
15 (12) The business entity has been convicted of a felony or class A
16 misdemeanor, or substantially similar offense in another jurisdiction,
17 related to the practice of the healing arts.
18 (h) The state board of healing arts shall adopt all rules and regulations
19 as necessary to implement and administer the provisions of this section.
20 (i) For the purposes of determining the impact on the healthcare
21 stabilization fund of requiring business entities to comply with the
22 provisions of the healthcare provider insurance availability act, the
23 healthcare stabilization fund is hereby directed to conduct such acturarial
24 actuarial and operational studies as are necessary to determine such
25 impact, and to report the findings to the legislature on or before January 1,
26 2020.
27 (j) This section shall be a part of and supplemental to the Kansas
28 healing arts act.
29 (k) The provisions of this section shall take effect on and after March
30 1, 2020.
31 Sec. 3. K.S.A. 17-2710 and K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 65-28,134 are hereby
32 repealed.
33 Sec. 4. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
34 publication in the statute book.
