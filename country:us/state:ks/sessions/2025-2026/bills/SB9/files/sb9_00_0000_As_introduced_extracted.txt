Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Transportation
1-15
1 AN ACT concerning United States public land surveys; relating to reports
2 filed with the state historical society; extending the time to file such
3 reports from 30 to 90 days; amending K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 58-2011 and
4 repealing the existing section.
5
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
7 Section 1. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 58-2011 is hereby amended to read as
8 follows: 58-2011. (a) Whenever a survey originates from a United States
9 public land survey corner or any related accessory, the land surveyor shall
10 file a reference report for each corner or accessory with the secretary of the
11 state historical society and with the county surveyor for the county or
12 counties in which the survey corner exists. If there is no county surveyor
13 of such county, such reference report shall be filed with the county
14 engineer. If there is no county engineer, such report shall be filed in the
15 office of the county road department or in a county office designated by
16 the county commission. Reports filed with the secretary of the state
17 historical society may be filed and retrieved using electronic technologies
18 if authorized by the secretary. Such report shall be filed within 30 90 days
19 of the date the references are made. At the time of filing such report with
20 the secretary of the state historical society, the land surveyor shall pay a
21 filing fee in an amount fixed by rules and regulations of the secretary of
22 the state historical society. Fees charged for filing and retrieval of such
23 reports may be billed and paid periodically.
24 (b) Any person engaged in an activity in which a United States public
25 land survey corner or any related accessory is likely to be altered,
26 removed, damaged or destroyed, shall have a person qualified to practice
27 land surveying establish such reference points as necessary for the
28 restoration, reestablishment or replacement of the corner or accessory. The
29 land surveyor shall file a reference report with the secretary of the state
30 historical society and with the county surveyor for the county or counties
31 in which the survey corner exists. Such report shall be filed within 30 90
32 days of the date the references are made. At the time of filing such report
33 with the secretary of the state historical society, the land surveyor shall pay
34 a filing fee in an amount fixed by rules and regulations of the secretary of
35 the state historical society.
36 (c) Upon completion of the activity likely to alter, remove, damage or
SB 9 2
1 destroy the public land survey corner or related accessory, the land
2 surveyor shall review the survey corner and its accessories. If the survey
3 corner or any accessory has been altered, removed, damaged or destroyed,
4 the land surveyor shall replace the corner or accessory with a survey
5 monument and file a restoration report with the secretary of the state
6 historical society and the county surveyor in the county or counties in
7 which it existed. If the survey corner and accessories are not damaged
8 during the activity, a restoration report so stating shall be filed with the
9 secretary of the state historical society and county surveyor's office. Such
10 report shall be filed within 30 90 days after the activity is completed. At
11 the time of filing such report with the office of the secretary of the state
12 historical society the land surveyor shall pay a filing fee in an amount
13 fixed by rules and regulations of the secretary of the state historical
14 society.
15 (d) Failure to comply with the filing requirements of this section shall
16 be grounds for the suspension or revocation of the land surveyor's license.
17 (e) The secretary of the state historical society may produce,
18 reproduce and sell maps, plats, reports, studies and records relating to land
19 surveys. The secretary of the state historical society shall charge a fee in an
20 amount to be fixed by rules and regulations of the secretary for the
21 furnishing of information retrieved from records filed pursuant to this
22 section and for reproductions or copies of maps, plats, reports, studies and
23 records filed in such office.
24 (f) All moneys collected by the secretary of the state historical society
25 under the provisions of this section shall be remitted to the state treasurer
26 in accordance with the provisions of K.S.A. 75-4215, and amendments
27 thereto. Upon receipt of each such remittance, the state treasurer shall
28 deposit the entire amount in the state treasury. Ten percent of each such
29 deposit shall be credited to the state general fund and the balance shall be
30 credited to the land survey fee fund, which is hereby created. All
31 expenditures from such fund shall be made in accordance with
32 appropriation acts upon warrants approved by the secretary of the state
33 historical society or a person designated by the secretary of the state
34 historical society and shall be used only for the purpose of paying the costs
35 incurred in administering the provisions of this act. After the effective date
36 of this act, any reference to the secretary of state in regard to
37 appropriations to the land survey fee fund shall be deemed to refer to the
38 secretary of the state historical society.
39 (g) The failure of any person to have a land surveyor establish
40 reference points as required by subsection (b) shall be a class C
41 misdemeanor.
42 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 58-2011 is hereby repealed.
43 Sec. 3. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
SB 9 3
1 publication in the statute book.
