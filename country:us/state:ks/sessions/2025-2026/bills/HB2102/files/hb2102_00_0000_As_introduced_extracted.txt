Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Education
Requested by Representative Chauncey
1-27
1 AN ACT concerning education; relating to school districts; providing for
2 the advance enrollment of military students whose parent or person
3 acting as parent will be stationed in this state; amending K.S.A. 2024
4 Supp. 72-3128 and repealing the existing section.
5
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
7 Section 1. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 72-3128 is hereby amended to read as
8 follows: 72-3128. (a) Except as otherwise provided in subsection
9 subsections (b) and (c), in each school year, if a school district has open
10 seats remaining after completion of the nonresident student transfer
11 application process established pursuant to K.S.A. 72-3123, and
12 amendments thereto, the board of education of the school district may
13 consider applications for enrollment submitted by students who are
14 residents of another state. The board of education of the school district
15 shall give priority to nonresident students who reside in Kansas over those
16 students who reside in another state prior to considering applications
17 submitted by students who are residents of another state.
18 (b) If a student who is a resident of another state has a parent or
19 person acting as parent employed by a school district in this state, the
20 board of education of such school district may permit such student to
21 enroll in and attend the school district as if the student is a resident of the
22 school district.
23 (c) A military student, as defined in K.S.A. 72-5139, and amendments
24 thereto, who provides evidence that such student's parent or person acting
25 as parent will be stationed at a military installation in this state during the
26 current or immediately succeeding school year, shall be permitted to
27 enroll in the school district where such student will reside in advance of
28 such student's physical residency in this state.
29 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 72-3128 is hereby repealed.
30 Sec. 3. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
31 publication in the statute book.
