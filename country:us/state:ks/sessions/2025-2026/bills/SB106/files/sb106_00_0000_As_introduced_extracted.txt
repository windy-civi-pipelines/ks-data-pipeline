Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Federal and State Affairs
1-29
1 AN ACT concerning veterans; relating to forms of identification;
2 permitting homeless veterans to use alternative forms of proof of
3 identity and residency when applying for nondriver identification cards;
4 eliminating fees for homeless veterans to obtain birth certificates for
5 purposes of nondriver identification card applications; amending
6 K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 8-1324 and 65-2418 and repealing the existing
7 sections.
8
9 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
10 Section 1. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 8-1324 is hereby amended to read as
11 follows: 8-1324. (a) Any resident who does not hold a current valid Kansas
12 driver's license may make application to the division of vehicles and be
13 issued one identification card.
14 (b) (1) Each application for an identification card shall include a
15 question asking if the applicant is willing to give such applicant's
16 authorization to be listed as an organ, eye and tissue donor in the Kansas
17 donor registry in accordance with the revised uniform anatomical gift act,
18 K.S.A. 65-3220 through 65-3244, and amendments thereto. The gift would
19 become effective upon the death of the donor.
20 (2) For the purpose of obtaining an identification card, an applicant
21 shall submit, with the application, proof of age, proof of identity and proof
22 of lawful presence. An applicant shall submit with the application a photo
23 identity document, except that a non-photo identity document is acceptable
24 if it includes both the applicant's full legal name and date of birth, and,
25 except as provided in subsection (k)(2), documentation showing the
26 applicant's name, the applicant's address of principal residence and the
27 applicant's social security account number. The applicant's social security
28 number shall remain confidential and shall not be disclosed, except as
29 provided pursuant to K.S.A. 74-2014, and amendments thereto. If the
30 applicant does not have a social security number, the applicant shall
31 provide proof of lawful presence and Kansas residency. The division shall
32 assign a distinguishing number to the identification card. Before issuing an
33 identification card to a person, the division shall make reasonable efforts to
34 verify with the issuing agency the issuance, validity and completeness of
35 each document required to be presented by the applicant to prove age,
36 identity and lawful presence.
SB 106 2
1 (c) The division shall not issue an identification card to any person
2 who fails to provide proof that the person is lawfully present in the United
3 States. If an applicant provides evidence of lawful presence as set out in
4 K.S.A. 8-240(b)(2)(E) through (2)(I), and amendments thereto, or is an
5 alien lawfully admitted for temporary residence under K.S.A. 8-240(b)(2)
6 (B), and amendments thereto, the division may only issue a temporary
7 identification card to the person under the following conditions:
8 (1) A temporary identification card issued pursuant to this
9 subparagraph subsection shall be valid only during the period of time of
10 the applicant's authorized stay in the United States or, if there is no definite
11 end to the period of authorized stay, a period of one year;
12 (2) a temporary identification card issued pursuant to this
13 subparagraph subsection shall clearly indicate that it is temporary and shall
14 state the date upon which it expires;
15 (3) no temporary identification card issued pursuant to this
16 subparagraph subsection shall be for a longer period of time than the time
17 period permitted by K.S.A. 8-1325, and amendments thereto; and
18 (4) a temporary identification card issued pursuant to this
19 subparagraph subsection may be renewed, subject at the time of renewal,
20 to the same requirements and conditions set forth in this subsection for the
21 issuance of the original temporary identification card.
22 (d) The division shall not issue an identification card to any person
23 who holds a current valid Kansas driver's license unless such driver's
24 license has been physically surrendered pursuant to the provisions of
25 K.S.A. 8-1002(e), and amendments thereto.
26 (e) The division shall refuse to issue an identification card to a person
27 holding a driver's license or identification card issued by another state
28 without confirmation that the person is terminating or has terminated the
29 license or identification card.
30 (f) The parent or guardian of an applicant under 16 years of age shall
31 sign the application for an identification card submitted by such applicant.
32 (g) (1) The division shall require payment of a fee of $14 at the time
33 application for an identification card is made, except that persons who are
34 65 or more years of age or who are handicapped, as defined in K.S.A. 8-
35 1,124, and amendments thereto, shall be required to pay a fee of only $10.
36 In addition to the fees prescribed by this subsection, the division shall
37 require payment of the photo fee established pursuant to K.S.A. 8-243, and
38 amendments thereto, for the cost of the photograph to be placed on the
39 identification card.
40 (2) The division shall not require or accept payment of application or
41 photo fees under this subsection for any person 17 years of age or older for
42 purposes of meeting the voter identification requirements of K.S.A. 25-
43 2908, and amendments thereto. Such person shall:
SB 106 3
1 (A) Swear under oath that such person desires an identification card
2 in order to vote in an election in Kansas and that such person does not
3 possess any of the forms of identification acceptable under K.S.A. 25-
4 2908, and amendments thereto. The affidavit shall specifically list the
5 acceptable forms of identification under K.S.A. 25-2908, and amendments
6 thereto; and
7 (B) produce evidence that such person is registered to vote in Kansas.
8 (3) The secretary of revenue shall adopt rules and regulations in order
9 to implement the provisions of paragraph (2).
10 (h) All Kansas identification cards shall have physical security
11 features designed to prevent tampering, counterfeiting or duplication for
12 fraudulent purposes.
13 (i) For the purposes of K.S.A. 8-1324 through 8-1328, and
14 amendments thereto, a person shall be deemed to be a resident of the state
15 if the person:
16 (1) Owns, leases or rents a place of domicile in this state;
17 (2) engages in a trade, business or profession in this state;
18 (3) is registered to vote in this state;
19 (4) enrolls the person's child in a school in this state; or
20 (5) registers the person's motor vehicle in this state.
21 (j) The division shall require that any person applying for an
22 identification card submit to a mandatory facial image capture. The
23 captured facial image shall be displayed on the front of the applicant's
24 identification card by either:
25 (1) A digital color image or photograph; or
26 (2) a laser-engraved photograph of the licensee.
27 (k) (1) Any person who is a veteran may request that the division
28 issue to such person a nondriver identification card that shall include the
29 designation "VETERAN" displayed on the front of the nondriver
30 identification card at a location to be determined by the secretary of
31 revenue. In order to receive a nondriver identification card described in
32 this subsection, the veteran shall provide a copy of the veteran's DD form
33 214, NGB form 22 or equivalent discharge document showing character of
34 service as honorable or general under honorable conditions.
35 (2) (A) Any person who is a veteran and is homeless may apply for
36 and be issued an identification card pursuant to this section without
37 providing an address of principal residence.
38 (B) In addition to any documents proving identity and residency in
39 Kansas accepted for an application for an identification card pursuant to
40 this section, a homeless veteran may provide and the division shall accept
41 a letter or certificate from the Kansas office of veterans services, the
42 Kansas soldiers' home, the Kansas veterans' home, any veterans services
43 medical center located in Kansas, any jail or correctional facility or any
SB 106 4
1 nonprofit organization located in Kansas for the provision of services for
2 the homeless verifying such applicant resides at or receives services from
3 such entity, and either:
4 (i) Copies or digital forms of accepted documents for proof of identity
5 in lieu of original documents; or
6 (ii) certain expired forms of identification, including, but not limited
7 to, common access cards, Kansas driver's licenses or Kansas voter
8 registration cards.
9 (3) As used in this subsection, "veteran" means a person who served
10 in the active military, naval, air or space service and who was discharged
11 or released therefrom under an honorable discharge or a general discharge
12 under honorable conditions.
13 (3)(4) The director of vehicles may adopt any rules and regulations
14 necessary to carry out the provisions of this subsection.
15 (l) The director of vehicles may issue a temporary identification card
16 to an applicant who cannot provide valid documentary evidence as defined
17 by subsection (c), if the applicant provides compelling evidence proving
18 current lawful presence. Any temporary identification card issued pursuant
19 to this subparagraph shall be valid for one year.
20 (m) Upon payment of the required fee, the division shall issue to
21 every applicant qualifying under the provisions of this act an identification
22 card. Such identification card shall bear a distinguishing number assigned
23 to the cardholder, the full legal name, date of birth, address of principal
24 residence, except as provided in subsection (k)(2), a brief description of
25 the cardholder, either:
26 (1) A digital color image or photograph; or
27 (2) a laser-engraved photograph of the cardholder, and a facsimile of
28 the signature of the cardholder. An identification card that does not contain
29 the address of principal residence of the cardholder as required may be
30 issued to persons who are program participants pursuant to K.S.A. 75-455,
31 and amendments thereto, or persons who meet the qualifications and
32 apply for an identification card under the conditions described in
33 subsection (k)(2).
34 (n) An identification card issued to any person who indicated on the
35 application that the person wished to make an anatomical gift in
36 accordance with the revised uniform anatomical gift act, K.S.A. 65-3220
37 through 65-3244, and amendments thereto, shall have the word "Donor"
38 placed on the front of the applicant's identification card.
39 (o) (1) Any person who submits satisfactory proof to the director of
40 vehicles, on a form provided by the director, that such person needs
41 assistance with cognition, including, but not limited to, persons with
42 autism spectrum disorder, may request that the division issue to such
43 person a nondriver identification card, that shall note such impairment on
SB 106 5
1 the nondriver identification card at a location to be determined by the
2 secretary of revenue.
3 (2) Satisfactory proof that a person needs assistance with cognition
4 shall include a statement from a person licensed to practice the healing arts
5 in any state, an advanced practice registered nurse licensed under K.S.A.
6 65-1131, and amendments thereto, a licensed physician assistant or a
7 person clinically licensed by the Kansas behavioral sciences regulatory
8 board certifying that such person needs assistance with cognition.
9 (p) The secretary of revenue shall permit an electronic online renewal
10 of an identification card if the electronic online renewal applicant
11 previously provided documentation of identity, lawful presence and
12 residence to the division for electronic scanning. For purposes of this
13 subsection, the division may rely on the division's most recent, existing
14 color digital image and signature image of the applicant for the nondriver's
15 identification card if the division has such images on file. The
16 determination on whether an electronic online renewal application or
17 equivalent of a nondriver's identification card is permitted shall be made
18 by the director of vehicles or the director's designee. The division shall not
19 renew a nondriver's identification card through an electronic online or
20 equivalent process if the identification card has been previously renewed
21 through an electronic online application in the immediately preceding
22 card's expiration period. No renewal under this subsection shall be granted
23 to any person who is a registered offender pursuant to K.S.A. 22-4901 et
24 seq., and amendments thereto.
25 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 65-2418 is hereby amended to read as
26 follows: 65-2418. (a) (1) The secretary shall fix and charge by rules and
27 regulations the fees to be paid for certified copies or abstracts of
28 certificates or for search of the files for birth, death, fetal death, marriage
29 or divorce records when no certified copy or abstract is made. Except as
30 otherwise provided in this section, the secretary shall remit all moneys
31 received by or for the secretary from fees, charges or penalties, under the
32 uniform vital statistics act, and amendments thereto, to the state treasurer
33 in accordance with the provisions of K.S.A. 75-4215, and amendments
34 thereto. Upon receipt of each such remittance, the state treasurer shall
35 deposit the entire amount in the state treasury to the credit of the civil
36 registration and health statistics fee fund created by K.S.A. 65-2418e, and
37 amendments thereto.
38 (2) The secretary shall not charge any fee for a certified copy of a
39 certificate or abstract or for a search of the files or records if the certificate,
40 abstract or search is requested by a person who exhibits correspondence
41 from the United States department of veterans affairs or the Kansas office
42 of veterans services that indicates that the person is applying for benefits
43 from the United States department of veterans affairs and that such person
SB 106 6
1 needs the requested information to obtain such benefits, except that, for a
2 second or subsequent certified copy of a certificate, abstract or search of
3 the files requested by the person, the usual fee shall be charged. The
4 secretary may provide by rules and regulations for exemptions from such
5 fees.
6 (3) The secretary shall not charge or accept any fee for a certified
7 copy of a certificate or abstract or for a search of the files or records if the
8 certificate, abstract or search is requested by a homeless veteran who
9 exhibits correspondence from the Kansas office of veterans services, the
10 Kansas soldiers' home, the Kansas veterans' home, any veterans services
11 medical center located in Kansas, any jail or correctional facility or any
12 nonprofit organization located in Kansas for the provision of services for
13 the homeless indicating that the person is applying for a nondriver
14 identification card pursuant to K.S.A. 8-1324, and amendments thereto,
15 and that such person needs the requested information to apply for such
16 identification card. The secretary may provide by rules and regulations for
17 exemption from such fees. For purposes of this paragraph, "veteran"
18 means the same as defined in K.S.A. 8-1324, and amendments thereto.
19 (4) The secretary shall not charge or accept any fee for a certified
20 copy of a birth certificate if the certificate is requested by any person who
21 is 17 years of age or older for purposes of voting if the applicant lacks the
22 identification required by K.S.A. 25-2908(h), and amendments thereto, or
23 to meet the voter registration requirements of K.S.A. 25-2309, and
24 amendments thereto. For voter registration purposes, an applicant for
25 registration shall swear under oath: (1) That such person plans to register
26 to vote in Kansas; and (2) that such person does not possess any of the
27 documents that constitute evidence of United States citizenship under
28 K.S.A. 25-2309(l), and amendments thereto. The affidavit shall
29 specifically list the documents that constitute evidence of United States
30 citizenship under K.S.A. 25-2309(l), and amendments thereto. The
31 secretary shall adopt rules and regulations in order to implement the
32 provisions of this subsection.
33 (4)(5) Upon receipt of any such remittance of a fee for a certified
34 copy of a birth certificate or abstract, $3 of each such fee for the first copy
35 of a birth certificate or abstract and $1 of each such fee for each additional
36 copy of the same birth certificate or abstract requested at the same time
37 shall be remitted to the state treasurer in accordance with the provisions of
38 K.S.A. 75-4215, and amendments thereto. Upon receipt of each such
39 remittance, the state treasurer shall deposit the entire amount in the state
40 treasury to the credit of the permanent families account of the family and
41 children investment fund created by K.S.A. 38-1808, and amendments
42 thereto. The balance of the money received for a fee for a certified copy of
43 a birth certificate or abstract shall be remitted to the state treasurer in
SB 106 7
1 accordance with the provisions of K.S.A. 75-4215, and amendments
2 thereto. Upon receipt of each such remittance, the state treasurer shall
3 deposit the entire amount in the state treasury to the credit of the civil
4 registration and health statistics fee fund created under this act.
5 (5)(6) Upon receipt of any such remittance of a fee for a certified
6 copy of a death certificate or abstract, $4 of each such fee for the first
7 certified copy of a death certificate or abstract and $2 of each such fee for

9 the same time shall be remitted to the state treasurer in accordance with the
10 provisions of K.S.A. 75-4215, and amendments thereto. Upon receipt of

15 be remitted to the state treasurer in accordance with the provisions of
16 K.S.A. 75-4215, and amendments thereto. Upon receipt of each such
17 remittance, the state treasurer shall deposit the entire amount in the state
18 treasury to the credit of the civil registration and health statistics fee fund
19 created by K.S.A. 65-2418e, and amendments thereto.
20 (b) Subject to K.S.A. 65-2415, and amendments thereto, the national
21 office of vital statistics may be furnished copies or data it requires for

23 the data. The data shall not be used for other than statistical purposes by
24 the national office of vital statistics unless so authorized by the state
25 registrar of vital statistics.
