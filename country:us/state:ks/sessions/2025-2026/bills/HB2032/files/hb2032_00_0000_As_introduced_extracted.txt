Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Energy, Utilities and Telecommunications
Requested by Paul Snider on behalf of Kansans for Lower Electric Rates
1-17
1 AN ACT concerning electric public utilities; relating to the state
2 corporation commission; authorizing the commission to increase or
3 decrease an electric public utility's return on equity based on whether
4 such utility's all-in average retail rate has increased or decreased;
5 requiring the commission to amend rates based on such changes;
6 defining all-in average retail rate.
7
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
9 Section 1. (a) Upon a showing by an electric public utility before the
10 state corporation commission at a public hearing and a finding by the
11 commission that such utility's all-in average retail rate has not increased
12 more than 1% in the preceding calendar year, the commission may order
13 an increase to such utility's return on equity in an amount up to 0.5%
14 above the return on equity authorized in such utility's most recent general
15 rate proceeding. Such increase shall be valid for the 12-months following
16 the issuance of such order.
17 (b) In a proceeding conducted pursuant to a complaint or the
18 commission's own motion, if the state corporation commission finds an
19 electric public utility's all-in average retail rate has increased more than
20 1% in the preceding calendar year, the commission may order a decrease
21 to such utility's return on equity in an amount up to 0.5% below the return
22 on equity authorized in such utility's most recent general rate proceeding.
23 Such decrease shall be valid for the 12-months following the issuance of
24 such order.
25 (c) The state corporation commission shall amend an electric public
26 utility's retail rates to include changes ordered pursuant to this section.
27 (d) As used in this section, "all-in average retail rate" means the total
28 cost of electricity a retail customer pays per kilowatt-hour and includes all
29 charges, fees, taxes, surcharges and customer charges.
30 Sec. 2. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
31 publication in the statute book.
