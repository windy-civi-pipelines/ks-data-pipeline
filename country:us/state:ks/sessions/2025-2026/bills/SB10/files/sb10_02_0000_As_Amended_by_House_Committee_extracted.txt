1 AN ACT concerning property taxation; relating to exemptions; providing
2 exemptions for certain personal property including watercraft, marine
3 equipment, off-road vehicles, motorized bicycles and, and certain
4 trailers and aircraft; amending K.S.A. 79-213 and 79-5501 and
5 repealing the existing sections.
6
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
8 New Section 1. (a) The following described property, to the extent
9 herein specified, is hereby exempt from all property or ad valorem taxes
10 levied under the laws of the state of Kansas:
11 (1) Any off-road vehicle that is not operated upon any highway;
12 (2) any motorized bicycle, electric-assisted bicycle, electric-assisted
13 scooter, electric personal assistive mobility device and motorized
14 wheelchair as such terms are defined in K.S.A. 8-126, and amendments
15 thereto;
16 (3) any trailer having a gross weight of 15,000 pounds or less that is
17 used exclusively for personal use and not for the production of income;
18 and and
19 (4) any marine equipment; and
20 (5) any aircraft.
21 (b) For purposes of this section:
22 (1) "Aircraft" means any airplane, helicopter or other machine
23 capable of flight;
24 (2) "Marine equipment" means any watercraft trailer designed to
25 launch, retrieve, transport and store watercraft and any watercraft motor
26 designed to operate watercraft on the water;
27 (2)(3)(2) "off-road motorcycle" means any motorcycle as defined in
28 K.S.A. 8-126, and amendments thereto, that has been manufactured for
29 off-road use only and is used exclusively off roads and highways; and
30 (3)(4)(3) "off-road vehicle" means:
31 (A) Any all-terrain vehicle, recreational off-highway vehicle and golf
32 cart as such terms are defined in K.S.A. 8-126, and amendments thereto;

1 and
2 (B) any off-road motorcycle and snowmobile.
3 (c) The provisions of this section shall apply to all taxable years
4 commencing after December 31, 2025.

7 valorem property taxes assessed, or to be assessed, against their property

10 appraiser.

12 the exemption is requested and state, in detail, the legal and factual basis
13 for the exemption claimed.

15 of the county where such property is principally located.

17 examination of the facts as alleged, the county appraiser shall recommend
18 that the exemption request either be granted or denied, and, if necessary,

29 appraiser and the county appraiser shall not file the request for exemption
30 and recommendations of the county appraiser with the state board of tax
31 appeals. The county clerk or county assessor shall annually make such
32 adjustment in the taxes levied against the real property as the owner may
33 be entitled to receive under the provisions of K.S.A. 79-201g, and
34 amendments thereto, as recommended by the county appraiser, beginning
35 with the first period, following the date of issue of the certificate of
36 completion on which taxes are regularly levied, and during the years
37 which the landowner is entitled to such adjustment.

39 the same and notify the applicant and the county appraiser of such fact.
40 (g) After examination of the request for exemption and the county
41 appraiser's recommendation related thereto, the board may fix a time and
42 place for hearing, and shall notify the applicant and the county appraiser of
43 the time and place so fixed. A request for exemption pursuant to: (1)
