Section 1:
(Corrected)
As Amended by House Committee
As Amended by Senate Committee
Session of 2025
By Committee on Education
1-27
1 AN ACT concerning education; relating to employees of school districts
2 and postsecondary educational institutions; enacting the given name act
3 to require such employees to use the name and pronouns consistent
4 with a student's biological sex and birth certificate; authorizing a cause
5 of action providing for the submission of civil complaints for
6 violations therefor.
7
8 WHEREAS, Employees of school districts and postsecondary
9 educational institutions do not shed their constitutional rights to freedom
10 of speech or expression at work; and
11 WHEREAS, Protecting the right to free speech for such employees
12 promotes the important interests of students receiving informed opinions
13 on matters of public concern, employee rights to disseminate their own
14 opinions and exposing our children and future leaders to different
15 viewpoints; and
16 WHEREAS, The selection and use of pronouns in classrooms, on
17 campuses and elsewhere is not merely an administrative or ministerial act,
18 but instead is a matter of free speech and academic freedom as it
19 communicates a message on a matter of public concern and shapes
20 classroom discussion and debate.
21 Now, therefore:
22 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
23 Section 1. (a) This section shall be known and may be cited as the
24 given name act.
25 (b) No employee of a school district or postsecondary educational
26 institution, regardless of such employee's official duties, shall address a
27 minor or student with a:
28 (1) Pronoun or title that is inconsistent with the biological sex of such
29 minor or student unless the employee has the written permission of such
30 minor's or student's parent; and
31 (2) name other than the name listed on the minor's or student's birth
32 certificate, or a derivative of such name, without the written permission of
33 the minor's or student's parent.
SB 76—Am. by HC 2
1 (c) No employee of a school district or postsecondary educational
2 institution shall be subject to an adverse employment action for declining
3 to address an individual using a name other than the name listed on the
4 individual's birth certificate or a pronoun or title that is inconsistent with
5 the individual's biological sex.
6 (d) No student shall be subject to any disciplinary action for declining
7 to address an individual using a name other than the name listed on the
8 individual's birth certificate or a pronoun or title that is inconsistent with
9 the individual's biological sex.
10 (e) A person who is harmed by a violation of this section may bring a
11 cause of action for injunctive relief, monetary damages, reasonable
12 attorney fees and any other appropriate reliefAny parent of a student
13 enrolled in a school district who is aggrieved by a violation of this
14 section may file a complaint alleging such violation with the board of
15 education of such school district. Such complaint shall be submitted in
16 writing and provide details of such violation. The board of education
17 shall appoint a committee to investigate any such complaints. The
18 committee shall meet with the complainant to discuss the complaint.
19 Within 30 days after receiving a complaint, the committee shall submit
20 a written recommended response and proposed steps to remedy the
21 complaint to the board of education. The board of education shall take
22 action on any such recommendations at the next regularly scheduled
23 meeting of the board. Nothing in this subsection shall be construed to
24 preclude any individual from pursuing any other remedies for a
25 violation of this section available at law.
26 (f) As used in this section:
27 (1) "Minor" means an unemancipated individual under 18 years of
28 age.
29 (2) "Parent" means natural parent, adoptive parent or person legally
30 authorized to act on behalf of the child.
31 (3) "Postsecondary educational institution" means the same as
32 defined in K.S.A. 74-3201b, and amendments thereto.
33 (4) "Student" means any unemancipated individual under 18 years of
34 age who is enrolled in and attending a public school or postsecondary
35 educational institution.
36 Sec. 2. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
37 publication in the statute book.
