Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Federal and State Affairs
3-4
1 AN ACT concerning the state governmental ethics law; relating to state
2 officers and employees; providing for the treatment of the
3 reimbursement for expenses incurred for travel and activities in
4 attending conferences or events by certain specified nonprofit
5 organizations and discounted or free access to entertainment, sporting
6 events or other activities; amending K.S.A. 46-237 and 46-237a and
7 repealing the existing sections.
8
9 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
10 Section 1. K.S.A. 46-237 is hereby amended to read as follows: 46-
11 237. (a) Except as provided by this section, no state officer or employee,
12 candidate for state office or state officer elect shall accept, or agree to
13 accept any:
14 (1) Economic opportunity, gift, loan, gratuity, special discount, favor,
15 hospitality or service having an aggregate value of $40 or more in any
16 calendar year; or
17 (2) hospitality in the form of recreation having an aggregate value of
18 $100 or more in any calendar year from any one person known to have a
19 special interest, under circumstances where such person knows or should
20 know that a major purpose of the donor is to influence such person in the
21 performance of their official duties or prospective official duties.
22 (b) Except as provided by this section, no person with a special
23 interest shall offer, pay, give or make any:
24 (1) Economic opportunity, gift, loan, gratuity, special discount, favor,
25 hospitality or service having an aggregate value of $40 or more in any
26 calendar year; or
27 (2) hospitality in the form of recreation having an aggregate value of
28 $100 or more in any calendar year to any state officer or employee,
29 candidate for state office or state officer elect with a major purpose of
30 influencing such officer or employee, candidate for state office or state
31 officer elect in the performance of official duties or prospective official
32 duties or to a member or member elect or employee of the judicial branch
33 with a major purpose of influencing the member or member elect or
34 employee of the judicial branch in the performance of official duties or
35 prospective official duties pertaining to a judicial administrative matter, as
36 defined in K.S.A. 46-225, and amendments thereto.
SB 285 2
1 (c) No person licensed, inspected or regulated by a state agency shall
2 offer, pay, give or make any economic opportunity, gift, loan, gratuity,
3 special discount, favor, hospitality or service having an aggregate value of
4 $40 or more in any calendar year to such agency or any state officer or
5 employee, candidate for state office or state officer elect of that agency.
6 (d) Hospitality in the form of food and beverages is presumed not to
7 be given to influence a state officer or employee, candidate for state office
8 or state officer elect in the performance of official duties or prospective
9 official duties, or to influence a member or member elect or employee of
10 the judicial branch in the performance of official duties or prospective
11 official duties pertaining to a judicial administrative matter as defined in
12 K.S.A. 46-225, and amendments thereto, except when a particular course
13 of official action is to be followed as a condition thereon.
14 (e) Except when a particular course of official action is to be followed
15 as a condition thereon, this section shall not apply to:
16 (1) Any contribution reported in compliance with the campaign
17 finance act; or
18 (2) a commercially reasonable loan or other commercial transaction
19 in the ordinary course of business.
20 (f) No state officer or employee shall accept any payment of
21 honoraria for any speaking engagement except that a member of the state
22 legislature or a part-time officer or employee of the executive branch of
23 government shall be allowed to receive reimbursement in the preparation
24 for and the making of a presentation at a speaking engagement in an
25 amount fixed by the commission prior to the acceptance of the speaking
26 engagement. Nothing in this section shall be construed to prohibit the
27 reimbursement of state officers and employees for reasonable expenses
28 incurred in attending seminars, conferences and other speaking
29 engagements.
30 (g) The provisions of this section shall not be applicable to or prohibit
31 the acceptance of gifts from governmental agencies of foreign nations
32 except that any gift accepted from such foreign governmental agency,
33 having an aggregate value of $100 or more, shall be accepted on behalf of
34 the state of Kansas.
35 (h) No legislator shall solicit any contribution to be made to any
36 organization for the purpose of paying for travel, subsistence and other
37 expenses incurred by such legislator or other members of the legislature in
38 attending and participating in meetings, programs and activities of such
39 organization or those conducted or sponsored by such organization, but
40 nothing in this act or the act of which this act is amendatory shall be
41 construed to prohibit any legislator from accepting reimbursement for
42 actual expenses for travel, subsistence, hospitality, entertainment and other
43 expenses incurred in attending and participating in meetings, programs and
SB 285 3
1 activities sponsored by the government of any foreign nation, or any
2 organization organized under the laws of such foreign nation or any
3 international organization or any national, nonprofit, nonpartisan
4 organization established for the purpose of serving, informing, educating
5 and strengthening state legislatures in all states of the nation that does not
6 engage in lobbying in the state of Kansas, when paid from funds of such
7 organization and nothing shall be construed to limit or prohibit the
8 expenditure of funds of and by any such organization for such purposes.
9 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 46-237a is hereby amended to read as follows: 46-
10 237a. (a) The provisions of this section shall apply to:
11 (1) The governor;
12 (2) the lieutenant governor;
13 (3) the governor's spouse;
14 (4) all officers and employees of the executive branch of state
15 government; and
16 (5) all members of boards, commissions and authorities of the
17 executive branch of state government.
18 (b) No person subject to the provisions of this section shall solicit or
19 accept any gift, economic opportunity, loan, gratuity, special discount or
20 service provided because of such person's official position, except:
21 (1) A gift having an aggregate value of less than $40 given at a
22 ceremony or public function where the person is accepting the gift in such
23 person's official capacity;
24 (2) gifts from relatives or gifts from personal friends when it is
25 obvious to the person that the gift is not being given because of the
26 person's official position;
27 (3) anything of value received by the person on behalf of the state
28 that inures to the benefit of the state or that becomes the property of the
29 state; or
30 (4) contributions solicited on behalf of a nonprofit organization which
31 that is exempt from taxation under paragraph (3) of subsection (c) of
32 section 501(c)(3) of the internal revenue code of 1986, as amended.
33 (c) No person subject to the provisions of this section shall solicit or
34 accept free or special discount meals from a source outside of state
35 government, except:
36 (1) Meals, the provision of which is motivated by a personal or
37 family relationship or provided at events that are widely attended. An
38 occasion is "widely attended" when it is obvious to the person accepting
39 the meal that the reason for providing the meal is not a pretext for
40 exclusive or nearly exclusive access to the person;
41 (2) meals provided at public events in which the person is attending
42 in an official capacity;
43 (3) meals provided to a person subject to this act when it is obvious
SB 285 4
1 such meals are not being provided because of the person's official position;
2 (4) food such as soft drinks, coffee or snack foods not offered as part
3 of a meal;
4 (5) any meal, the value of which is $40 or less, not provided by a
5 lobbyist registered pursuant to K.S.A. 46-265, and amendments thereto;
6 (6) meals provided to a person when the person's presence at the
7 event or meeting at which the meal is provided serves a legitimate state
8 purpose or interest and the agency of which such person is an officer or
9 employee authorizes such person's attendance at such event or meeting;
10 (7) meals provided to the governor's spouse and members of the
11 governor's immediate family at the event or meeting at which the meal is
12 provided serve a legitimate state purpose or interest; and
13 (8) any meal, if provided by a lobbyist registered pursuant to K.S.A.
14 46-265, and amendments thereto, and the lobbyist reports providing the
15 meal as required pursuant to K.S.A. 46-269, and amendments thereto,
16 except when a particular course of official action is to be followed as a
17 condition of accepting the meal.
18 (d) No person subject to the provisions of this section shall solicit or
19 accept free or special discount travel or related expenses from a source
20 outside state government, except:
21 (1) When it is obvious to the person accepting the same that the free
22 or special discount travel and related expenses are not being provided
23 because of the person's official position; or
24 (2) when the person's presence at a meeting, seminar or event serves a
25 legitimate state purpose or interest and the person's agency authorizes or
26 would authorize payment for such travel and expenses.
27 (e) (1) Except as provided by paragraph 2, no person subject to the
28 provisions of this section shall solicit or accept free or special discount
29 tickets or access to entertainment or sporting events or activities such as
30 plays, concerts, games, golf, exclusive swimming, hunting or fishing or
31 other recreational activities when the free or special discount tickets or
32 access are provided because of the person's official position, except:
33 (A) If it is obvious to the person accepting such free or special
34 discount tickets or access that the free or special discount tickets or access
35 are not being provided because of such person's official position; or
36 (B) if the person's presence at such event or activity serves a
37 legitimate state purpose or interest and such person's agency authorizes
38 or would authorize payment for such travel and expenses.
39 (2) The provisions of this subsection paragraph (1) shall not apply to
40 persons whose official position requires or obliges them to be present at
41 such events or activities.
42 (f) (1) Violations of the provisions of this section by any classified
43 employee in the civil service of the state of Kansas shall be considered
SB 285 5
1 personal conduct detrimental to the state service and shall be a basis for
2 suspension, demotion or dismissal, subject to applicable state law.
3 (2) Violations of the provisions of this section by any unclassified
4 employee shall subject such employee to discipline up to and including
5 termination.
6 (3) In addition to the penalty prescribed under paragraphs (1) and (2),
7 the commission may assess a civil fine, after proper notice and an
8 opportunity to be heard, against any person for a violation of this section,
9 in an amount not to exceed $5,000 for the first violation, not to exceed
10 $10,000 for the second violation and not to exceed $15,000 for the third
11 violation and for each subsequent violation. All fines assessed and
12 collected under this section shall be remitted to the state treasurer in
13 accordance with the provisions of K.S.A. 75-4215, and amendments
14 thereto. Upon receipt of each such remittance, the state treasurer shall
15 deposit the entire amount in the state treasury to the credit of the
16 governmental ethics fee fund established by K.S.A. 25-4119e, and
17 amendments thereto.
18 (4) Receiving a meal provided by a lobbyist who is not registered
19 pursuant to K.S.A. 46-265, and amendments thereto, or who fails to report
20 providing the meal as required pursuant to K.S.A. 46-269, and
21 amendments thereto, or as required by subsection (c)(8), shall not be
22 considered a violation of this section, unless the recipient knew the
23 lobbyist was not registered or requested that the lobbyist not report the
24 meal.
25 Sec. 3. K.S.A. 46-237 and 46-237a are hereby repealed.
26 Sec. 4. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
27 publication in the statute book.
