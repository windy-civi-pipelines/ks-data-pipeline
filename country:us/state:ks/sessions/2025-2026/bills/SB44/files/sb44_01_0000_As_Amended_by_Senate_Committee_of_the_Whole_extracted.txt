Section 1:
{As Amended by Senate Committee of the Whole}
Session of 2025
By Committee on Education
1-21
1 AN ACT concerning education; relating to the Kansas promise scholarship
2 program; expanding the postsecondary educational institutions eligible
3 to participate in such program; amending K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,271
4 and 74-32,274 and repealing the existing sections.
5
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
7 Section 1. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,271 is hereby amended to read as
8 follows: 74-32,271. (a) K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,271 et seq., and
9 amendments thereto, shall be known and may be cited as the Kansas
10 promise scholarship act.
11 (b) As used in the Kansas promise scholarship act:
12 (1) "Eligible postsecondary educational institution" means:
13 (A) Any community college or technical college established under
14 the laws of this state and with a recognized service area;
15 (B) the Washburn institute of technology; or
16 (C) any not-for-profit institution of postsecondary education with its
17 main campus or principal place of operation in Kansas that offers a
18 promise eligible program, is operated independently and not controlled or
19 administered by any state agency or subdivision of the state, maintains
20 open enrollment and is accredited by a nationally recognized accrediting
21 agency for higher education in the United States; or
22 (D) any institution of postsecondary education with its main campus
23 or principal place of operation in Kansas that offers a promise eligible
24 program, is recognized by the state board of regents, is accredited by a
25 nationally recognized accrediting agency for higher education in the
26 United States and is eligible to receive funding under title IV of the higher
27 education act of 1965.
28 (2) "Military servicemember" means the same as defined in K.S.A.
29 2024 Supp. 48-3406, and amendments thereto.
30 (3) "Part-time student" means a student who is enrolled for six credit
31 hours or more in the fall, summer or spring semester and is not enrolled as
32 a full-time student.
33 (4) "Promise eligible program" means any two-year associate degree
34 program or career and technical education certificate or stand-alone
35 program offered by an eligible postsecondary educational institution that
36 is:
SB 44—Am. by SCW 2
1 (A) Approved by the state board of regents;
2 (B) high wage, high demand or critical need; and
3 (C) identified as a "promise eligible program" by the state board of
4 regents pursuant to K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,272, and amendments
5 thereto, or a "promise eligible program" within a field of study designated
6 by an eligible postsecondary educational institution pursuant to K.S.A.
7 2024 Supp. 74-32,273, and amendments thereto.
8 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,274 is hereby amended to read as
9 follows: 74-32,274. (a) Subject to appropriations, the amount of a Kansas
10 promise scholarship for a student for each academic year shall be
11 determined as follows:
12 (1) For a student enrolled in a promise eligible program offered by an
13 eligible public postsecondary educational institution described in K.S.A.
14 2024 Supp. 74-32,271(b)(1)(A) or (B), and amendments thereto, the
15 scholarship amount shall be the aggregate amount of tuition, required fees
16 and the cost of books and required materials for the promise eligible
17 program at the eligible postsecondary educational institution for the
18 academic year in which the student is enrolled and receiving the
19 scholarship minus the aggregate amount of all other aid awarded to such
20 student for such academic year.
21 (2) For a student enrolled in a promise eligible program offered by an
22 eligible private postsecondary educational institution described in K.S.A.
23 2024 Supp. 74-32,271(b)(1)(C) or (D), and amendments thereto, the
24 scholarship amount shall be the aggregate amount of tuition, required fees
25 and the cost of books and materials for such program for the academic
26 year in which the student is enrolled and receiving the scholarship minus
27 the aggregate amount of all other aid awarded to such student for such
28 academic year, except that a scholarship awarded pursuant to this
29 paragraph shall not exceed the average cost of tuition, required fees and
30 the cost of books and required materials for such promise eligible program
31 when offered by an eligible public postsecondary educational institution
32 described in K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,271(b)(1)(A) or (B), and
33 amendments thereto.
34 (b) Kansas promise scholarships shall only be awarded to an eligible
35 student whose family household income equals $100,000 or less for a
36 family of one or two, $150,000 or less for a family of three and, for
37 household sizes above three, a household income that is equal to or less
38 than the family of three amount plus $4,800 for each additional family
39 member.
40 (c) (1) Kansas promise scholarship awards shall be used only to pay
41 for up to a total of 68 promise scholarship funded credit hours or a total of
42 $20,000 in Kansas promise scholarship awards, whichever occurs first,
43 over the lifetime of the student who received the Kansas promise
SB 44—Am. by SCW 3
1 scholarship award regardless of the eligible postsecondary educational
2 institution such student attended.
3 (2) Kansas promise scholarship awards shall not be used to fund:
4 (A) Prerequisite classes required for a promise eligible program
5 unless such classes are a designated course within the eligible program; or
6 (B) any remedial course, as defined in K.S.A. 76-7,151, and
7 amendments thereto, unless such course is offered in a corequisite format.
8 (d) For each fiscal year, the appropriation made for the Kansas
9 promise scholarship program shall not exceed $10,000,000 {$15,000,000}.
10 (e) The state board of regents shall disburse funds based on
11 reimbursement requests from eligible postsecondary educational
12 institutions. Reimbursement requests shall be based on the actual amount
13 of Kansas promise scholarship amounts awarded by an eligible
14 postsecondary educational institution for the appropriate academic period.
15 Any eligible postsecondary educational institution seeking reimbursement
16 shall submit a reimbursement request to the state board of regents on or
17 before September 1, December 1, March 1 and June 1 of each year. The
18 state board of regents shall disburse the appropriate amount of funds to
19 eligible postsecondary educational institutions on September 15,
20 December 15, March 15 and June 15 each year.
21 (f) As used in this section, "aid" includes any grant, scholarship or
22 financial assistance awards that do not require repayment. "Aid" does not
23 include any military financial educational benefits or any family
24 postsecondary savings account or other qualified tuition program
25 established pursuant to section 529 of the internal revenue code of 1986, as
26 amended.
27 Sec. 3. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 74-32,271 and 74-32,274 are hereby
28 repealed.
29 Sec. 4. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
30 publication in the statute book.
