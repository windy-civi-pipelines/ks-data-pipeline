Section 1:
As Amended by House Committee
As Amended by Senate Committee
Session of 2025
By Committee on Education
1-30
1 AN ACT concerning education; relating to certain ancillary school district
2 activities; authorizing nonpublic school students and virtual school
3 students to participate in such activities; making it unlawful for a
4 school district or the state high school activities association to
5 discriminate against such students based on enrollment status;
6 amending K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 72-7121 and repealing the existing
7 section.
8
9 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
10 Section 1. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 72-7121 is hereby amended to read as
11 follows: 72-7121. (a) (1) Any student who meets the requirements of this
12 section shall be permitted to participate in any activities offered by a
13 school district that are regulated, supervised, promoted and developed by
14 the activities association referred to in K.S.A. 72-7114, and amendments
15 thereto.
16 (2) Participation in such activities includes participation in ancillary
17 activities, events and programs associated with any activity described in
18 paragraph (1). Ancillary activities include, but are not limited to, pep
19 rallies, award or honor ceremonies, lettering events, yearbook or other
20 promotional functions and other events or programs promoting an activity
21 described in paragraph (1) or honoring students who participate in any
22 such activityAny student participating in an activity pursuant to
23 paragraph (1) shall also be permitted to participate in any district-
24 sponsored events, ceremonies, programs or other functions directly
25 related to such activity.
26 (3) The board of education of a school district may adopt policies
27 regarding the participation of those students who are participating in
28 an activity pursuant to paragraph (1) in district-sponsored events,
29 ceremonies, programs or other functions that are not directly related
30 to such activity.
31 (3)(b) A student shall be permitted to participate in any such activities
32 if such student:
33 (1)(1) (A) Is a resident of the school district;
34 (2)(B) is enrolled and attending a virtual school as defined in K.S.A.
SB 114—Am. by HC 2
1 72-3712, and amendments thereto, or a nonpublic elementary or secondary
2 school;
3 (3)(C) complies with the requirements of K.S.A. 72-6262, and
4 amendments thereto, prior to participation in any such activity;
5 (4)(D) meets any applicable age and eligibility requirements set forth
6 by the activities association referred to in K.S.A. 72-7114, and
7 amendments thereto, that are not otherwise in conflict with this section;
8 (5)(E) pays any fees required by the school district for participation in
9 such activity if such fees are generally imposed upon all other students
10 who participate in such activity; and
11 (6)(F) seeks participation at the appropriate school of the school
12 district that corresponds to where such student resides within the school
13 district's respective school attendance boundaries established by the board
14 of education of the school district; or
15 (2) (A) Is enrolled in and attending the Kansas academy of
16 mathematics and science as defined in K.S.A. 72-3903, and amendments
17 thereto;
18 (B) complies with the requirements of K.S.A. 72-6262, and
19 amendments thereto, prior to participation in any such activity;
20 (C) meets any applicable age and eligibility requirements set forth
21 by the activities association referred to in K.S.A. 72-7114, and
22 amendments thereto, that are not otherwise in conflict with this section;
23 (D) pays any fees required by the school district for participation in
24 such activity if such fees are generally imposed upon all other students
25 who participate in such activity; and
26 (E) seeks participation at the appropriate school of the school
27 district that corresponds to where the postsecondary educational
28 institution designated by the state board of regents for the Kansas
29 academy of mathematics and science program.
30 (b)(c) (1) Any student attending a home school who seeks to
31 participate in an activity in the student's resident school district shall be
32 deemed to meet any academic eligibility requirements established by the
33 activities association for participation in an activity if:
34 (A) The student is maintaining satisfactory progress towards
35 achievement or promotion to the next grade level; and
36 (B) the parent, teacher or organization that provides instruction to the
37 student submits an affidavit or transcript to the activities association
38 indicating the student meets the academic eligibility requirements of
39 subparagraph (A).
40 (2) Upon submission of an affidavit, the student attending a home
41 school shall be deemed to meet any academic eligibility requirements
42 established by the activities association and shall retain such academic
43 eligibility during the activity season for which such affidavit is submitted.
SB 114—Am. by HC 3
1 (c)(d) Except as provided in subsection (d) (e), a student attending a
2 virtual school as defined in K.S.A. 72-3712, and amendments thereto, who
3 seeks to participate in an activity in the student's resident school district
4 shall not be required to enroll in or attend a minimum number of courses at
5 such school district.
6 (d)(e) The board of education of a school district may require a
7 student who participates in an activity pursuant to this section to enroll in a
8 particular course or complete a particular course as a condition of
9 participation, if such requirement is imposed upon all other students who
10 participate in such activity.
11 (e)(f) Except as provided in subsection (b) (c), any student who seeks
12 to participate in an activity pursuant to this section shall be subject to any
13 tryout or other participation requirements that are otherwise applicable to
14 all other students for participation in the activity.
15 (f)(g) Any student enrolled in a school operated by a school
16 district who withdraws from such school district and subsequently
17 enrolls in an accredited private school, a nonpublic elementary or
18 secondary school, as defined in K.S.A. 72-4345, and amendments
19 thereto, or a virtual school, as defined in K.S.A. 72-3712, and
20 amendments thereto, shall not be eligible for full participation in any
21 activities offered by such school district for the nine-week period
22 immediately following such student's withdrawal unless such student
23 was eligible for full participation in any such activities pursuant to the
24 eligibility policies of such school district and the activities association
25 referred to in K.S.A. 72-7114, and amendment thereto, on the date of
26 withdrawal and such student participates in such activities at the
27 school from which such student withdrew. Such student may be
28 permitted limited participation in any such activities in accordance
29 with the eligibility policies of such school district and the activities
30 association referred to in K.S.A. 72-7114, and amendments thereto.
31 (h) Any student who meets the requirements of this section and
32 participates in activities described in subsection (a) shall be entitled to all
33 rights and subject to all responsibilities of any other participating student,
34 except as otherwise provided in this section, regardless of such student's
35 enrollment status.
36 (g)(h)(i) It shall be unlawful for any school district or the activities
37 association referred to in K.S.A. 72-7114, and amendments thereto, to
38 discriminate against any student who meets the requirements of this
39 section based on such student's enrollment status.
40 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 72-7121 is hereby repealed.
41 Sec. 3. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
42 publication in the statute book Kansas register.
