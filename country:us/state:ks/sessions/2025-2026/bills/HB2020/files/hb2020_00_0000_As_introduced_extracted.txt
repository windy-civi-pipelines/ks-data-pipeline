Session of 2025
By Committee on Elections

1-16
1 AN ACT concerning driver's licenses of noncitizens; requiring the director
2 of the division of motor vehicles to make monthly reports of names and
3 addresses of noncitizens to the secretary of state; amending K.S.A. 8-
4 240 and repealing the existing section.
5
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
7 New Section 1. The director of the division of motor vehicles shall
8 provide a list of all permanent and temporary drivers' licenses issued to
9 noncitizens quarterly and forward this list to the secretary of state. The list
10 shall contain the names, addresses, phone numbers, social security
11 numbers, dates of birth, temporary drivers' license numbers and expiration
12 dates of such licenses. The secretary of state shall compare such lists with
13 the voter registration rolls and delete any names of noncitizens that appear
14 on the voter rolls. The secretary of state shall notify any person removed
15 from the voter registration rolls that the person may be reinstated on the
16 voter registration rolls by providing proof of their citizenship.
17 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 8-240 is hereby amended to read as follows: 8-240. (a)
18 (1) Every application for an instruction permit shall be made upon a form
19 furnished by the division of vehicles and accompanied by a fee of $2 for
20 class A, B, C or M and $5 for all commercial classes. Every other
21 application shall be made upon a form furnished by the division and
22 accompanied by an examination fee of $3, unless a different fee is required
23 by K.S.A. 8-241, and amendments thereto, and by the proper fee for the
24 license for which the application is made. All commercial class applicants
25 shall be charged a $15 driving test fee for the drive test portion of the
26 commercial driver's license application. If the applicant is not required to
27 take an examination or the commercial license drive test, the examination
28 or commercial drive test fee shall not be required. The examination shall
29 consist of three tests, as follows: (A) Vision; (B) written; and (C) driving.
30 For a commercial driver's license, the drive test shall consist of three
31 components, as follows: (A) Pre-trip; (B) skills test; and (C) road test. If
32 the applicant fails the vision test, the applicant may have correction of
33 vision made and take the vision test again without any additional fee. If an
34 applicant fails the written test, the applicant may take such test again upon
35 the payment of an additional examination fee of $1.50. If an applicant fails

1 the driving test, the applicant may take such test again upon the payment
2 of an additional examination fee of $1.50. If an applicant for a commercial
3 driver's license fails any portion of the commercial drive test, the applicant
4 may take such test again upon the payment of an additional drive test fee
5 of $10. If an applicant fails to pass all three of the tests within a period of
6 six months from the date of original application and desires to take
7 additional tests, the applicant shall file an application for reexamination
8 upon a form furnished by the division, which shall be accompanied by a
9 reexamination fee of $3, except that any applicant who fails to pass the
10 written or driving portion of an examination four times within a six-month
11 period, shall be required to wait a period of six months from the date of the
12 last failed examination before additional examinations may be given.
13 Upon the filing of such application and the payment of such reexamination
14 fee, the applicant shall be entitled to reexamination in like manner and
15 subject to the additional fees and time limitation as provided for
16 examination on an original application. If the applicant passes the
17 reexamination, the applicant shall be issued the classified driver's license
18 for which the applicant originally applied, which license shall be issued to
19 expire as if the applicant had passed the original examination.
20 (2) Applicants for class M licenses who have completed prior
21 motorcycle safety training in accordance with department of defense
22 instruction 6055.04 (DoDI 6055.04) or the motorcycle safety foundation
23 are not required to complete further written and driving testing pursuant to
24 paragraph (1). An applicant seeking exemption from the written and
25 driving tests pursuant to this paragraph shall provide a copy of the
26 motorcycle safety foundation completion form to the division prior to
27 receiving a class M license.
28 (3) On and after January 1, 2017, an applicant for a class M license
29 who passes a driving examination on a three-wheeled motorcycle that is
30 not an autocycle shall have a restriction placed on such applicant's license
31 limiting the applicant to the operation of a registered three-wheeled
32 motorcycle. An applicant for a class M license who passes a driving
33 examination on a two-wheeled motorcycle may operate any registered
34 two-wheeled or three-wheeled motorcycle. The driving examination
35 required by this paragraph shall be administered by the division, by the
36 department of defense or as part of a curriculum recognized by the
37 motorcycle safety foundation.
38 (b) (1) For the purposes of obtaining any driver's license or
39 instruction permit, an applicant shall submit, with the application, proof of
40 age and proof of identity as the division may require. The applicant also
41 shall provide a photo identity document, except that a non-photo identity
42 document is acceptable if it includes both the applicant's full legal name
43 and date of birth, and documentation showing the applicant's name, the

3 and shall not be disclosed, except as provided pursuant to K.S.A. 74-2012,
4 and amendments thereto. If the applicant does not have a social security
5 number, the applicant shall provide proof of lawful presence and Kansas
6 residency. The division shall assign a distinguishing number to the license
7 or permit.
8 (2) The division shall not issue any driver's license or instruction
9 permit to any person who fails to provide proof that the person is lawfully
10 present in the United States. Before issuing a driver's license or instruction
11 permit to a person, the division shall require valid documentary evidence
12 that the applicant: (A) Is a citizen or national of the United States; (B) is an
13 alien lawfully admitted for permanent or temporary residence in the
14 United States; (C) has conditional permanent resident status in the United
15 States; (D) has an approved application for asylum in the United States or
16 has entered into the United States in refugee status; (E) has a valid,
17 unexpired nonimmigrant visa or nonimmigrant visa status for entry into
18 the United States; (F) has a pending application for asylum in the United
19 States; (G) has a pending or approved application for temporary protected
20 status in the United States; (H) has approved deferred action status; or (I)
21 has a pending application for adjustment of status to that of an alien
22 lawfully admitted for permanent residence in the United States or
23 conditional permanent resident status in the United States.
24 (3) If an applicant provides evidence of lawful presence set out in
25 subsections (b)(2)(E) through (2)(I), or is an alien lawfully admitted for
26 temporary residence under subsection (b)(2)(B), the division may only
27 issue a driver's license to the person under the following conditions: (A) A
28 driver's license issued pursuant to this subparagraph shall be valid only
29 during the period of time of the applicant's authorized stay in the United
30 States or, if there is no definite end to the period of authorized stay, a
31 period of one year; (B) a driver's license issued pursuant to this
32 subparagraph shall clearly indicate that it is temporary and shall state the
33 date on which it expires; (C) no driver's license issued pursuant to this
34 subparagraph shall be for a longer period of time than the time period
35 permitted by K.S.A. 8-247(a), and amendments thereto; and (D) a driver's
36 license issued pursuant to this subparagraph may be renewed, subject at
37 the time of renewal, to the same requirements and conditions as set out in
38 this subsection (b) for the issuance of the original driver's license.
39 (4) The division shall not issue any driver's license or instruction
40 permit to any person who is not a resident of the state of Kansas, except as
41 provided in K.S.A. 8-2,148, and amendments thereto.
42 (5) The division shall not issue a driver's license to a person holding a
43 driver's license issued by another state without making reasonable efforts

6 gender and address of principal residence of the applicant, and briefly
7 describe the applicant, and shall state whether the applicant has been
8 licensed as a driver prior to such application, and, if so, when and by what
9 state or country. Such application shall state whether any such license has

21 organ, eye or tissue donor in the Kansas donor registry in accordance with
22 the revised uniform anatomical gift act, K.S.A. 65-3220 through 65-3244,
23 and amendments thereto. The gift would become effective upon the death
24 of the donor.
25 (d) When an application is received from a person previously licensed
26 in another jurisdiction, the division shall request a copy of the driver's
27 record from the other jurisdiction. When received, the driver's record shall
28 become a part of the driver's record in this state with the same force and
29 effect as though entered on the driver's record in this state in the original
30 instance.
31 (e) When the division receives a request for a driver's record from
32 another licensing jurisdiction the record shall be forwarded without charge.

34 (1) For a class C driver's license issued to a person at least 21 years of

36 (2) for a class C driver's license issued to a person 65 years of age or
37 older, $12;
38 (3) for a class M driver's license issued to a person at least 21 years of
39 age, but less than 65 years of age, $12.50;
40 (4) for a class M driver's license issued to a person 65 years of age or
41 older, $9;
42 (5) for a class A or B driver's license issued to a person who is at least
43 21 years of age, but less than 65 years of age, $24;

1 (6) for a class A or B driver's license issued to a person 65 years of
2 age or older, $16;
3 (7) for any class of commercial driver's license issued to a person 21
4 years of age or older, $18; or
5 (8) for class A, B, C or M, or a farm permit, or any commercial
6 driver's license issued to a person less than 21 years of age, $20.
7 A fee of $10 shall be charged for each commercial driver's license
8 endorsement, except air brake endorsements, which shall have no charge.
9 A fee of $3 per year shall be charged for any renewal of a license issued
10 prior to the effective date of this act to a person less than 21 years of age.
11 If one fails to make an original application or renewal application for a
12 driver's license within the time required by law, or fails to make
13 application within 60 days after becoming a resident of Kansas, a penalty
14 of $1 shall be added to the fee charged for the driver's license.
15 (g) Any person who possesses an identification card as provided in
16 K.S.A. 8-1324, and amendments thereto, shall surrender such
17 identification card to the division upon being issued a valid Kansas driver's
18 license or upon reinstatement and return of a valid Kansas driver's license.
19 (h) The division shall require that any person applying for a driver's
20 license submit to a mandatory facial image capture. The captured facial
21 image shall be displayed on the front of the applicant's driver's license.
22 (i) The director of vehicles may issue a temporary driver's license to
23 an applicant who cannot provide valid documentary evidence as defined
24 by subsection (b)(2), if the applicant provides compelling evidence
