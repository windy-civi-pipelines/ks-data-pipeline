Section 1:
As Amended by Senate Committee
Session of 2025
By Committee on Public Health and Welfare
2-4
1 AN ACT concerning health and healthcare; relating to healthcare
2 professions; updating the definition of athletic trainer; providing for an
3 exception for those licensed in another state, District of Columbia,
4 territory or foreign country to practice in Kansas; amending K.S.A. 65-
5 6902, 65-6906 and 65-6907 and repealing the existing sections.
6
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
8 Section 1. K.S.A. 65-6902 is hereby amended to read as follows: 65-
9 6902. As used in this act:
10 (a) "Board" means the state board of healing arts.
11 (b) "Athletic training" means the practice of injury of injury
12 prevention, physical evaluation, emergency care and referral or physical
13 reconditioning relating to athletic activity and wellness promotion, risk
14 management, immediate or emergency care, examination, assessment and
15 therapeutic intervention or rehabilitation of injury and illness, physical
16 evaluation, emergency care and referral or physical reconditioning
17 relating to athletic activity including, but not limited to, sports
18 participation, exercise, fitness training, strength and conditioning work,
19 recreational physical activities and competitive athletics. "Athletic
20 training" encompasses wellness promotion, risk management,
21 immediate or emergency care, examination, assessment and therapeutic
22 intervention or rehabilitation of athletic injury and illness. Athletic
23 training also means making clinical decisions to determine if consultation
24 or referrals are necessary, healthcare administration, professional
25 responsibility, performance of athletic training research and the education
26 of educating and consulting with the public regarding safe participation
27 in athletic activities and proper training methods.
28 (c) "Athletic trainer" means a person licensed under this act.
29 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 65-6906 is hereby amended to read as follows: 65-
30 6906. (a) Applications for licensure as an athletic trainer shall be made in
31 writing to the board on a form and in the manner prescribed by the board.
32 Each application shall be accompanied by the required fee, which shall not
33 be refundable. Each application shall contain such information necessary
34 to enable the board to judge the qualifications of the applicant for
35 licensure.
36 (b) The applicant is entitled to licensure as an athletic trainer if the
SB 175—Am. by SC 2
1 applicant possesses the qualifications set forth under K.S.A. 65-6907, and
2 amendments thereto, pays the licensure fee established by the board and
3 has not committed an act which that constitutes ground for denial of
4 licensure.
5 (c) The board may issue a license as an athletic trainer without
6 examination to an applicant:
7 (1) Who presents evidence satisfactory to the board of being licensed,
8 registered or certified in another state, District of Columbia, territory or
9 foreign country and of having passed an examination in athletic training
10 before a similarly lawfully authorized examining board in athletic training
11 of another state, District of Columbia, territory or foreign country if the
12 standards for the examination and for licensure, registration or certification
13 in athletic training in such other state, District of Columbia, territory or
14 foreign country are determined by the board to be at least equivalent to
15 those of this state; or
16 (2) who presents evidence satisfactory to the board of having been
17 engaged in the practice of athletic training in another state, District of
18 Columbia, territory or foreign country and passed an examination in
19 athletic training by the national athletic trainers' association board of
20 certification, inc. or other recognized national voluntary credentialing
21 body, which examination the board finds is at least equivalent to the
22 examination approved by the board under K.S.A. 65-6907, and
23 amendments thereto, and who is certified by the national athletic trainers'
24 association board of certification, inc. or other recognized national
25 voluntary credentialing body, which certification the board finds was
26 issued based on standards at least equivalent to the standards for licensure
27 as an athletic trainer in this state; and
28 (3) who, at the time of making such application has not been subject
29 to discipline or does not have a disciplinary action pending resulting from
30 the practice of athletic training in another state, District of Columbia,
31 territory or foreign country; and
32 (4) who, at the time of making such application, pays to the board a
33 fee as prescribed, no part of which shall be returned.
34 (d) As a condition of performing the functions and duties of an
35 athletic trainer in this state, each licensed athletic trainer shall file a
36 practice protocol with the board. The practice protocol shall be signed by
37 each person licensed by the board to practice the healing arts who will
38 delegate to the athletic trainer acts which constitute athletic training and
39 shall contain such information as required by rules and regulations adopted
40 by the board.
41 (e) The board may issue a temporary permit to an applicant for
42 licensure as an athletic trainer who meets the requirements for licensure as
43 an athletic trainer as required by K.S.A. 65-6907, and amendments thereto,
SB 175—Am. by SC 3
1 or who meets all the requirements for licensure except examination and
2 who pays to the board the temporary permit fee as required under K.S.A.
3 65-6910, and amendments thereto. Such temporary permit shall expire six
4 months from the date of issue or on the date that the board approves or
5 denies the application for licensure, whichever occurs first. No more than
6 one such temporary permit shall be granted to any one person.
7 (f) An individual who accompanies an athletic team or organization
8 from another state or jurisdiction and provides the services of an athletic
9 trainer in Kansas is exempt from the licensure requirements of the Kansas
10 athletic trainers licensure act, provided that the person is:
11 (1) Licensed and able to practice as an athletic trainer in another
12 state, District of Columbia, territory or foreign country; and
13 (2) limited to providing the services of an athletic trainer to
14 members of the athletic team or organization that traveled to Kansas.
15 Sec. 3. K.S.A. 65-6907 is hereby amended to read as follows: 65-
16 6907. An applicant for licensure as an athletic trainer shall give proof that
17 the applicant has:
18 (a) Received a baccalaureate or post-baccalaureate degree with a
19 major course of study in an athletic training curriculumGraduated after
20 successful completion of the curriculum requirements of an accredited
21 athletic training education program at an accredited college or university
22 approved by the board; and
23 (b) passed an examination in athletic training approved by the board.
24 Sec. 4. K.S.A. 65-6902, 65-6906 and 65-6907 are hereby repealed.
25 Sec. 5. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
26 publication in the statute book.
