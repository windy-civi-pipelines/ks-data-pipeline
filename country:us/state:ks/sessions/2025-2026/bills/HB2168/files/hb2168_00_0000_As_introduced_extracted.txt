Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Transportation
Requested by Zach Denney on behalf of the Department of Revenue
1-30
1 AN ACT concerning motor vehicles; relating to license plates; providing
2 for the blackout distinctive license plate; creating the license plate
3 replacement fund to be administered by the secretary of revenue;
4 depositing the fees from the blackout license plate into such fund for
5 purposes of replacing exhausted license plates.
6
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
8 Section 1. (a) On and after January 1, 2026, any owner or lessee of
9 one or more passenger vehicles or trucks registered for a gross weight of
10 20,000 pounds or less, who is a resident of Kansas, upon compliance with
11 the provisions of this section, may be issued one blackout license plate for
12 each such passenger vehicle or truck. Such license plate shall be issued for
13 the same period of time as other license plates upon proper registration and
14 payment of the regular license fee as provided in K.S.A. 8-143, and
15 amendments thereto.
16 (b) Any applicant or renewal of registration for a blackout license
17 plate authorized by this section shall pay an annual fee of $50 to the
18 county treasurer for each such license plate to be issued. Any new blackout
19 license plate shall be subject to the fee prescribed in K.S.A 8-1,141(a)(1),
20 and amendments thereto. A blackout license plate may be a personalized
21 license plate and subject to the personalized license plate fee pursuant to
22 K.S.A. 8-1,141(a)(2), and amendments thereto.
23 (c) Any applicant for a license plate authorized by this section may
24 make application for such license plate on a form prescribed and furnished
25 by the director of vehicles. Application for registration of a passenger
26 vehicle or truck and issuance of the license plate under this section shall be
27 made by the owner or lessee in a manner prescribed by the director of
28 vehicles upon forms furnished by the director.
29 (d) No registration or license plate issued under this section shall be
30 transferable to any other person.
31 (e) The director of vehicles may transfer a blackout license plate from
32 a leased vehicle to a purchased vehicle.
33 (f) Renewals of registration under this section shall be made annually,
34 upon payment of the fee prescribed in K.S.A. 8-143, and amendments
35 thereto, and in the manner prescribed in K.S.A. 8-132, and amendments
HB 2168 2
1 thereto. No renewal of registration shall be made to any applicant until
2 such applicant has paid the fee provided in subsection (b). If such fee is
3 not paid, such applicant shall be required to comply with K.S.A. 8-143,
4 and amendments thereto, and return the license plate to the county
5 treasurer of such applicant's residence.
6 (g) The blackout license plate shall have a background design, an
7 emblem or colors that designate the license plate as a blackout license
8 plate.
9 (h) There is hereby established in the state treasury the license plate
10 replacement fund. The license plate replacement fund shall be
11 administered by the secretary of revenue. All expenditures from such fund
12 shall be for the costs associated with replacing license plates when the
13 lifespan of such license plates has been exhausted as determined by the
14 director of vehicles. All expenditures from such fund shall be made in
15 accordance with appropriation acts upon warrants of the director of
16 accounts and reports issued pursuant to vouchers approved by the
17 secretary of revenue or the secretary's designee.
18 Sec. 2. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
19 publication in the statute book.
