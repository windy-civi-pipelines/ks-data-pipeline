As Amended by House Committee
As Amended by Senate Committee
Session of 2025
By Committee on Assessment and Taxation
1-16
1 AN ACT concerning motor vehicles; relating to distinctive license plates;
2 providing for the hunter nation distinctive license plate; decreasing the
3 guarantee number of distinctive plates required for production;
4 amending K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 8-1,141 and repealing the existing
5 section; also repealing K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 8-1,141a.
6
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
8 New Section 1. (a) On and after January 1, 2026, any owner or lessee
9 of one or more passenger vehicles or trucks registered for a gross weight
10 of 20,000 pounds or less who is a resident of Kansas, upon compliance
11 with the provisions of this section, may be issued one hunter nation license
12 plate for each such passenger vehicle or truck. Such license plate shall be
13 issued for the same time as other license plates upon proper registration
14 and payment of the regular license fee as provided in K.S.A. 8-143, and
15 amendments thereto, and the payment to the county treasurer of the logo
16 use royalty payment.
17 (b) Hunter nation, inc., may authorize the use of the organization's
18 logo to be affixed on license plates as provided by this section. Any motor
19 vehicle owner or lessee shall pay an amount of not less than $25 nor more
20 than $100, as determined by hunter nation, inc., as a logo use royalty
21 payment for each such license plate to be issued. The logo use royalty
22 payment shall be paid to the county treasurer.
23 (c) Any applicant for a license plate authorized by this section may
24 make application for such license plate not less than 60 days prior to such
25 person's renewal of registration date, on a form prescribed and furnished
26 by the director of vehicles, and any applicant for such license plate shall
27 pay to the county treasurer the logo use royalty payment. Application for
28 registration of a passenger vehicle or truck and issuance of the license
29 plate under this section shall be made by the owner or lessee in a manner
30 prescribed by the director of vehicles upon forms furnished by the director.
31 (d) No registration or license plate issued under this section shall be
32 transferable to any other person.
33 (e) The director of vehicles may transfer a hunter nation license plate
34 from a leased vehicle to a purchased vehicle.
SB 18—Am. by HC 2
1 (f) Renewals of registration under this section shall be made annually,
2 upon payment of the fee prescribed in K.S.A. 8-143, and amendments
3 thereto, and in the manner prescribed in K.S.A. 8-132(b), and amendments
4 thereto. No renewal of registration shall be made to any applicant until
5 such applicant provides to the county treasurer the annual royalty payment.
6 If such annual royalty payment is not made to the county treasurer, the
7 applicant shall be required to comply with the provisions of K.S.A. 8-143,
8 and amendments thereto, and return the license plate to the county
9 treasurer of such person's residence.
10 (g) Hunter nation, inc., with the approval of the director of vehicles,
11 shall design a plate to be issued under the provisions of this section.
12 (h) As a condition of receiving the hunter nation license plate and any
13 subsequent registration renewal of such license plate, the applicant shall
14 consent to the division authorizing the division's release of motor vehicle
15 record information, including the applicant's name, address, royalty
16 payment amount, plate number and vehicle type to hunter nation, inc., and
17 the state treasurer.
18 (i) The collection and remittance of annual royalty payments by the
19 county treasurer shall be subject to the provisions of K.S.A. 8-1,141(h),
20 and amendments thereto, except that payments from the hunter nation
21 license plate royalty fund shall be made on a monthly basis to hunter
22 nation foundation, inc.
23 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 8-1,141 is hereby amended to read as
24 follows: 8-1,141. (a) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2), any new
25 distinctive license plate authorized for issuance on and after July 1, 1994,
26 shall be subject to the personalized license plate fee prescribed by K.S.A.
27 8-132(d), and amendments thereto. This section shall not apply to any
28 distinctive license plate authorized prior to July 1, 1994.
29 (2) On and after January 1, 2025, any distinctive license plate may be
30 a personalized license plate subject to the provisions of K.S.A. 8-132, and
31 amendments thereto. Any personalized distinctive license plate shall be
32 subject to a fee that is double the amount prescribed by K.S.A. 8-132(d),
33 and amendments thereto.
34 (b) The director of vehicles shall not issue any new distinctive license
35 plate unless there is a guarantee of an initial issuance of at least 250 100
36 license plates.
37 (c) The provisions of this section shall not apply to distinctive license
38 plates issued under the provisions of K.S.A. 8-177d, 8-1,145, 8-1,163, 8-
39 1,166, 8-1,185, 8-1,186, 8-1,187, 8-1,188, 8-1,194, 8-1,195, 8-1,196, 8-
40 1,197, 8-1,198, 8-1,199, 8-1,204 or 8-1,205, and amendments thereto, or
41 K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 8-1,221 or 8-1,222, and amendments thereto, except
42 that such distinctive license plates may be personalized license plates
43 pursuant to subsection (a)(2) if an applicant pays the personalized license
SB 18—Am. by HC 3
1 plate fee prescribed by K.S.A. 8-132(d), and amendments thereto.
2 (d) The provisions of subsection (a) shall not apply to distinctive
3 license plates issued under the provisions of K.S.A. 8-1,146, 8-1,148, 8-
4 1,153, 8-1,158 or 8-1,161, and amendments thereto, except that such
5 distinctive license plates may be personalized license plates pursuant to
6 subsection (a)(2) if an applicant pays the personalized license plate fee
7 prescribed by K.S.A. 8-132(d), and amendments thereto.
8 (e) The provisions of subsection (f) shall not apply to distinctive
9 license plates issued under the provisions of K.S.A. 8-1,160, and 8-1,183,
10 and amendments thereto, and K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 8-1,211 and 8-1,219, and
11 amendments thereto, except that the division shall delay the manufacturing
12 and issuance of such distinctive license plate until the division has
13 received not fewer than 100 orders for such plate, including payment of
14 the personalized license plate fee required under subsection (a). Upon
15 certification by the director of vehicles to the director of accounts and
16 reports that not less than 100 paid orders for such plate have been
17 received, the director of accounts and reports shall transfer $4,000 from
18 the state highway fund to the distinctive license plate fund.
19 (f) (1) Any person or organization sponsoring any distinctive license
20 plate authorized by the legislature shall submit to the division of vehicles a
21 nonrefundable amount not to exceed $5,000, to defray the division's cost
22 for developing such distinctive license plate.
23 (2) All moneys received under this subsection shall be remitted by the
24 secretary of revenue to the state treasurer in accordance with the
25 provisions of K.S.A. 75-4215, and amendments thereto. Upon receipt of
26 each such remittance, the state treasurer shall deposit the entire amount in
27 the state treasury to the credit of the distinctive license plate fund which is
28 hereby created in the state treasury. All moneys credited to the distinctive
29 license plate fund shall be used by the department of revenue only for the
30 purpose associated with the development of distinctive license plates. All
31 expenditures from the distinctive license plate application fee fund shall be
32 made in accordance with appropriation acts, upon warrants of the director
33 of accounts and reports issued pursuant to vouchers approved by the
34 secretary of the department of revenue.
35 (g) The director of vehicles shall discontinue the issuance of any
36 distinctive license plate if:
37 (1) Fewer than 250 100 plates, including annual renewals, are issued
38 for that distinctive license plate by the end of the second year of sales; and
39 (2) fewer than 125 50 license plates, including annual renewals, are
40 issued for that distinctive license plate during any subsequent two-year
41 period.
42 (h) An application for any distinctive license plate issued and the
43 corresponding royalty fee may be collected either by the county treasurer

1 or the entity benefiting from the issuance of the distinctive license plate.
2 Annual royalty payments collected by the county treasurers shall be
3 remitted to the state treasurer in accordance with the provisions of K.S.A.
4 75-4215, and amendments thereto. Upon receipt of each such remittance
5 the state treasurer shall deposit the entire amount in the state treasury to
6 the credit of a segregated royalty fund which shall be administered by the
7 state treasurer. All expenditures from the royalty fund shall be made in
8 accordance with appropriation acts upon warrants of the director of
9 accounts and reports issued pursuant to vouchers approved by the state
10 treasurer or the state treasurer's designee. Payments from the royalty fund
11 shall be made to the entity benefiting from the issuance of the distinctive
12 license plate on a monthly basis.
13 (i) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, for any distinctive
14 license plate, the division shall produce such distinctive license plate for a
15 motorcycle upon request to the division by the organization sponsoring the
16 distinctive license plate.
17 (j) In addition to any residency requirements for all distinctive license
18 plates, any person not a resident of Kansas, serving as a member of the
19 armed forces stationed in this state shall be eligible to apply for any
20 distinctive license plate as if the individual was a resident of this state.
21 Such person shall be eligible to renew the distinctive license plate
22 registration as long as the person is still stationed in this state at the time
23 the registration is renewed.
24 Sec. 3. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 8-1,141 and 8-1,141a are hereby repealed.
25 Sec. 4. 2. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
26 publication in the statute book.
