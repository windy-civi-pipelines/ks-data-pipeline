Section 1:
As Amended by House Committee
Session of 2025
By Committee on Energy, Utilities and Telecommunications
Requested by Laura Lutz on behalf of Evergy
1-28
1 AN ACT concerning utilities; relating to liability for fire event damages;
2 providing for claims and recovery for economic damages; calculating
3 recovery for such limiting recovery of punitive damages; requiring the
4 state corporation commission to provide trainings convene a workshop
5 on wildfire risk and mitigation.
6
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
8 New Section 1. (a) As used in this section and section 2, and
9 amendments thereto:
10 (1) "Commission" means the state corporation commission.
11 (2) "Electric public utility" means a public utility as defined in K.S.A.
12 66-104, and amendments thereto, that is engaged in the generation,
13 transmission or distribution of electricity.
14 (3) "Fire claim" means any claim, whether based on negligence,
15 nuisance, trespass or any other claim for relief, brought by a person against
16 an electric public utility in a civil action to recover for damages resulting
17 from a fire event.
18 (4) "Fire event" means an uncontrolled or unplanned fire in the state
19 alleged to have been caused by an electric public utility.
20 (b) A fire claim shall be brought within two years of the date of the
21 ignition of damage from the fire event that is the subject of such claim.
22 (c) Subject to subsection (d) and (e), After an injured plaintiff
23 establishes by a preponderance of evidence that a loss was due to a fire
24 event caused by an electric public utility's conduct, such plaintiff bringing
25 a fire claim under this section may recover economic losses to compensate
26 for damage to property.
27 (d) An award for damages to real property resulting from a fire event
28 recoverable under subsection (c), shall be the lesser of:
29 (1) The cost to restore the property to the condition of such property
30 immediately before the fire event; or
31 (2) the difference between:
32 (A) The fair market value of the property immediately before the fire
33 event; or
34 (B) the fair market value of the property immediately after the fire
35 event.
HB 2107—Am. by HC 2
1 (e) Pursuant to K.S.A. 60-3701(e) 60-3702(e), and amendments
2 thereto, punitive damages awarded under a fire claim brought under
3 subsection (c) pursuant to this section shall not exceed $5,000,000.
4 Sec. 2. On or before July 31, 2026, the state corporation commission
5 shall provide training opportunities convene a workshop to assess
6 wildfire risk and mitigation. Such trainings workshop shall provide a
7 forum for the presentation and discussion of the following information:
8 (a) General wildfire risks in the state;
9 (b) utility readiness to mitigate wildfire risks;
10 (c) risk mitigation strategies and approaches; and
11 (d) cost recovery treatment for wildfire mitigation costs, including
12 investments and expenses.
13 Sec. 3. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
14 publication in the statute book.
