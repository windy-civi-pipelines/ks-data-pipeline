Section 1:
{As Amended by Senate Committee of the Whole}
As Amended by Senate Committee
As Amended by House Committee
Session of 2025
By Committee on Energy, Utilities and Telecommunications
Requested by Laura Lutz on behalf of Evergy
1-28
1 AN ACT concerning utilities; relating to liability for fire event damages;
2 providing for claims and recovery for economic damages; calculating
3 recovery for such limiting recovery of punitive damages; requiring the
4 state corporation commission to provide trainings convene a workshop
5 on wildfire risk and mitigation and authorizing the commission to
6 open a general investigation or convene additional workshops to
7 further assess wildfire risk and mitigation.
8
9 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
10 New Section 1. (a) As used in this section and section 2, and
11 amendments thereto:
12 (1) "Commission" means the state corporation commission.
13 (2) "Electric public utility" means a public utility as defined in K.S.A.
14 66-104, and amendments thereto, that is engaged in the generation,
15 transmission or distribution of electricity.
16 (3) "Fire claim" means any claim, whether based on negligence,
17 nuisance, trespass or any other claim for relief, brought by a person against
18 an electric public utility in a civil action to recover for damages resulting
19 from a fire event.
20 (4) "Fire event" means an uncontrolled or unplanned fire in the state
21 alleged to have been caused by an electric public utility.
22 (b) A fire claim shall be brought within two years of the date of the
23 ignition of damage from the fire event that is the subject of such claim
24 {or, if the fact of injury is not reasonably ascertainable until some time
25 after the initial act, then the period of limitation shall not commence
26 until the fact of injury becomes reasonably ascertainable to the injured
27 party, except that in no event shall an action be commenced more than
28 10 years beyond the fire event}.
29 (c) Subject to subsection (d) and (e), After an injured plaintiff
30 establishes by a preponderance of evidence that a loss was due to a fire
31 event caused by an electric public utility's conduct, such plaintiff bringing
HB 2107—Am. by SCW 2
1 a fire claim under this section may recover economic losses and
2 noneconomic damages to compensate for damage to property any such
3 loss.
4 (d) An award for damages to real property resulting from a fire event
5 recoverable under subsection (c), shall be the lesser of:
6 (1) The cost to restore the property to the condition of such property
7 immediately before the fire event; or
8 (2) the difference between:
9 (A) The fair market value of the property immediately before the fire
10 event; or
11 (B) the fair market value of the property immediately after the fire
12 event.
13 (e) Pursuant to K.S.A. 60-3701(e) 60-3702(e), and amendments
14 thereto, punitive damages awarded under a fire claim brought under
15 subsection (c) pursuant to this section shall not exceed $5,000,000.
16 Sec. 2. (a) On or before July 31, 2026, the state corporation
17 commission shall provide training opportunities convene a workshop to
18 assess wildfire risk and mitigation. Such trainings workshop shall provide
19 a forum for the presentation and discussion of the following
20 information:
21 (a)(1) General wildfire risks in the state;
22 (b)(2) utility readiness to mitigate wildfire risks;
23 (c)(3) risk mitigation strategies and approaches; and
24 (d)(4) cost recovery treatment for wildfire mitigation costs, including
25 investments and expenses.
26 (b) If determined necessary by the state corporation commission,
27 the commission may open a general investigation or convene additional
28 workshops to further assess utility wildfire risk and mitigation.
29 Sec. 3. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
30 publication in the statute book.
