Section 1:
{As Amended by House Committee of the Whole}
Session of 2025
By Committee on Judiciary
Requested by Representative L. Williams
1-22
1 AN ACT concerning children and families; relating to orders of child
2 support; providing for child support for unborn children from the date
3 of conception {and including the direct medical and pregnancy-
4 related expenses of the mother}; amending K.S.A. 20-165, 23-2205
5 and 23-3001 and repealing the existing sections.
6
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
8 Section 1. K.S.A. 20-165 is hereby amended to read as follows: 20-
9 165. (a) The supreme court shall adopt rules establishing guidelines for the
10 amount of child support to be ordered in any action in this state including,
11 but not limited to, K.S.A. 39-755 and K.S.A. 23-2215, and amendments
12 thereto, article 30 of chapter 23 of the Kansas Statutes Annotated, and
13 amendments thereto, and K.S.A. 23-2711, and amendments thereto.
14 (b) In adopting such rules, the court shall consider all relevant factors,
15 including, but not limited to:
16 (1) The needs of the child;
17 (2) the standards of living and circumstances of the parents;
18 (3) the relative financial means of the parents;
19 (4) the earning ability of the parents;
20 (5) the need and capacity of the child for education;
21 (6) the age of the child;
22 (7) the financial resources and earning ability of the child;
23 (8) the responsibility of the parents for the support of others; and
24 (9) the value of services contributed by both parents; and
25 (10) the direct medical and pregnancy-related expenses of the mother
26 if the child is an unborn child.
27 (c) The maximum amount of child support to be ordered for the
28 support of an unborn child shall not exceed the direct medical and
29 pregnancy-related expenses of the mother of the unborn child. Pregnancy-
30 related expenses shall not include any costs related to an elective
31 abortion.
32 (d) The amount of child support determined pursuant to subsection
33 (b)(10) shall be calculated from the date of conception of such unborn
34 {mother's} child. Interest shall accrue for any such amount of child
35 support at the statutory rate provided under K.S.A. 16-204, and
HB 2062—Am. by HCW 2
1 amendments thereto, and shall continue to accrue until the payment of
2 such child support is no longer in arrearage.
3 (e) As used in this section:
4 (1) "Elective abortion" means an abortion for any reason other than
5 to prevent the death of the mother upon whom the abortion is performed,
6 except that an abortion may not be deemed one to prevent the death of the
7 mother based on a claim or diagnosis that such mother will engage in
8 conduct that would result in such mother's death; and
9 (2) "unborn child" means the same as defined in K.S.A. 23-3001, and
10 amendments thereto.
11 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 23-2205 is hereby amended to read as follows: 23-
12 2205. (a) As used in this act, "parent and child relationship" means the
13 legal relationship existing between a child and the child's biological or
14 adoptive parents incident to which the law confers or imposes rights,
15 privileges, duties and obligations. It "Parent and child relationship"
16 includes the mother and child relationship and the father and child
17 relationship.
18 (b) For purposes of this section, the term "child" includes any unborn
19 child as defined in K.S.A. 23-3001, and amendments thereto.
20 Sec. 3. K.S.A. 23-3001 is hereby amended to read as follows: 23-
21 3001. (a) In any action under article 27 of chapter 23 of the Kansas
22 Statutes Annotated, and amendments thereto, the court shall make
23 provisions for the support and education of the minor children.
24 (b) Regardless of the type of custodial arrangement ordered by the
25 court, the court may order the child support and education expenses to be
26 paid by either or both parents for any child less than 18 years of age, at
27 which age the support shall terminate unless:
28 (1) The parent or parents agree, by written agreement approved by the
29 court, to pay support beyond the time the child reaches 18 years of age;
30 (2) the child reaches 18 years of age before completing the child's
31 high school education in which case the support shall not terminate
32 automatically, unless otherwise ordered by the court, until June 30 of the
33 school year during which the child became 18 years of age if the child is
34 still attending high school; or
35 (3) the child is still a bona fide high school student after June 30 of
36 the school year during which the child became 18 years of age, in which
37 case the court, on motion, may order support to continue through the
38 school year during which the child becomes 19 years of age so long as the
39 child is a bona fide high school student and the parents jointly participated
40 or knowingly acquiesced in the decision which that delayed the child's
41 completion of high school. The court, in extending support pursuant to
42 subsection (b)(3) this paragraph, may impose such conditions as are
43 appropriate and shall set the child support utilizing the guideline table
HB 2062—Am. by HCW 3
1 category for 12-year through 18-year-old children. For purposes of this
2 section, "bona fide high school student" means a student who is enrolled in
3 full accordance with the policy of the accredited high school in which the
4 student is pursuing a high school diploma or a graduate equivalency
5 diploma (GED).
6 (c) Provision for payment of support and educational expenses of a
7 child after reaching 18 years of age if still attending high school shall
8 apply to any child subject to the jurisdiction of the court, including those
9 whose support was ordered prior to July 1, 1992. If an agreement approved
10 by the court prior to July 1, 1992, provides for termination of support
11 before the date provided by subsection (b)(3), the court may review and
12 modify such agreement, and any order based on such agreement, to extend
13 the date for termination of support to the date provided by subsection (b)
14 (3)As used in article 30 of chapter 23 of the Kansas Statutes Annotated,
15 and amendments thereto, the term "unborn child" means a living
16 individual organism of the species homo sapiens, in utero, at any stage of
17 gestation from fertilization to birth.
18 Sec. 4. K.S.A. 20-165, 23-2205 and 23-3001 are hereby repealed.
19 Sec. 5. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
20 publication in the statute book.
