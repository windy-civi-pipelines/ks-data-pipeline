14 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 75-5702 is hereby amended to read as follows: 75-
15 5702. (a) The secretary of labor may appoint, with the consent of the
16 governor, one public information officer, one or more division directors,
17 one personal secretary and one special assistant, all of whom shall serve at
18 the pleasure of the secretary of labor, shall be in the unclassified service
19 under the Kansas civil service act and shall receive an annual salary fixed
20 by the secretary of labor with the approval of the governor.
21 (b) (1) The secretary may:
22 (A) Conduct public or private investigations within or outside of this
23 state which the secretary or the secretary's designee considers necessary or
24 appropriate to determine whether a person has violated, is violating, or is
25 about to violate the employment security law act or a rule and regulation
26 adopted or order issued under the employment security law, or to aid in the
27 enforcement of the employment security law;
28 (B) require or permit a person to testify, file a statement, or produce a
29 record, under oath or otherwise as the secretary or the secretary's designee
30 determines, as to all the facts and circumstances concerning a matter to be
31 investigated or about which an action or proceeding is to be instituted; and
32 (C) appoint one or more special investigators to aid in investigations
33 conducted pursuant to this act.
34 (2) Such special investigators shall have authority to make arrests,
35 serve subpoenas and all other process, conduct searches and seizures, store
36 evidence, and carry firearms, concealed or otherwise while investigating
37 violations of the employment security law act and to generally enforce all
38 the criminal laws of the state as violations of those laws are encountered
39 by such special investigators, except that no special investigator may carry
40 firearms while performing such duties without having first successfully
41 completed the training course prescribed for law enforcement officers
42 under the Kansas law enforcement training act, K.S.A. 74-5601 et seq.,
43 and amendments thereto.
