Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Health and Human Services
Requested by Representatives Buehler and McDonald on behalf of Casino
Employees Against Smoking Effects
2-4
1 AN ACT concerning the Kansas indoor clear air act; prohibiting smoking
2 on the gaming floor of a lottery gaming facility or racetrack gaming
3 facility; amending the definition of smoking to include the use of an
4 electronic cigarette and smoking of marijuana; amending K.S.A. 21-
5 6109 and 21-6110 and repealing the existing sections.
6
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
8 Section 1. K.S.A. 21-6109 is hereby amended to read as follows: 21-
9 6109. As used in K.S.A. 21-6109 through 21-6116, and amendments
10 thereto:
11 (a) "Access point" means the area within a ten foot radius outside of
12 any doorway, open window or air intake leading into a building or facility
13 that is not exempted pursuant to K.S.A. 21-6110(d), and amendments
14 thereto.
15 (b) "Bar" means any indoor area that is operated and licensed for the
16 sale and service of alcoholic beverages, including alcoholic liquor as
17 defined in K.S.A. 41-102, and amendments thereto, or cereal malt
18 beverages as defined in K.S.A. 41-2701, and amendments thereto, for on-
19 premises consumption.
20 (c) "Electronic cigarette" means the same as defined in the Kansas
21 cigarette and tobacco products act.
22 (d) "Employee" means any person who is employed by an employer
23 in consideration for direct or indirect monetary wages or profit and any
24 person who volunteers their services for a nonprofit entity.
25 (d)(e) "Employer" means any person, partnership, corporation,
26 association or organization, including municipal or nonprofit entities, that
27 employs one or more individual persons.
28 (e)(f) "Enclosed area" means all space between a floor and ceiling
29 that is enclosed on all sides by solid walls, windows or doorways that
30 extend from the floor to the ceiling, including all space therein screened by
31 partitions that do not extend to the ceiling or are not solid or similar
32 structures. For purposes of this section, the following shall not be
33 considered an "enclosed area": or windows, whether open or closed. A
34 wall includes any retractable divider, garage door or other physical
HB 2252 2
1 barrier, whether temporary or permanent and whether or not containing
2 openings of any kind
3 (1) Rooms or areas, enclosed by walls, windows or doorways, having
4 neither a ceiling nor a roof and that are completely open to the elements
5 and weather at all times; and
6 (2) rooms or areas, enclosed by walls, fences, windows or doorways
7 and a roof or ceiling, having openings that are permanently open to the
8 elements and weather and that comprise an area that is at least 30% of the
9 total perimeter wall area of such room or area.
10 (f)(g) "Food service establishment" means any place in which food is
11 served or is prepared for sale or service on the premises. Such term shall
12 include, but not be limited to, fixed or mobile restaurants, coffee shops,
13 cafeterias, short-order cafes, luncheonettes, grills, tea rooms, sandwich
14 shops, soda fountains, taverns, private clubs, roadside kitchens,
15 commissaries and any other private, public or nonprofit organization or
16 institution routinely serving food and any other eating or drinking
17 establishment or operation where food is served or provided for the public
18 with or without charge.
19 (g)(h) "Gaming floor" means the area of a lottery gaming facility or
20 racetrack gaming facility, as those terms are defined in K.S.A. 74-8702,
21 and amendments thereto, where patrons engage in Class III gaming. The
22 gaming floor shall not include any areas used for accounting, maintenance,
23 surveillance, security, administrative offices, storage, cash or cash
24 counting, records, food service, lodging or entertainment, except that the
25 gaming floor may include a bar where alcoholic beverages are served so
26 long as the bar is located entirely within the area where Class III gaming is
27 conducted.
28 (h)(i) "Marijuana" means the same as defined in K.S.A. 65-4101, and
29 amendments thereto.
30 (j) "Medical care facility" means a physician's office, general
31 hospital, special hospital, ambulatory surgery center or recuperation center,
32 as defined by K.S.A. 65-425, and amendments thereto, and any psychiatric
33 hospital licensed under K.S.A. 39-2001 et seq., and amendments thereto.
34 (i)(k) "Outdoor recreational facility" means a hunting, fishing,
35 shooting or golf club, business or enterprise operated primarily for the
36 benefit of its owners, members and their guests and not normally open to
37 the general public.
38 (j)(l) "Place of employment" means any enclosed area under the
39 control of a public or private employer, including, but not limited to, work
40 areas, auditoriums, elevators, private offices, employee lounges and
41 restrooms, conference and meeting rooms, classrooms, employee
42 cafeterias, stairwells and hallways, that is used by employees during the
43 course of employment. For purposes of this section, a private residence
HB 2252 3
1 shall not be considered a "place of employment" unless such residence is
2 used as a day care home, as defined in K.S.A. 65-530, and amendments
3 thereto.
4 (k)(m) "Private club" means an outdoor recreational facility operated
5 primarily for the use of its owners, members and their guests that in its
6 ordinary course of business is not open to the general public for which use
7 of its facilities has substantial dues or membership fee requirements for its
8 members.
9 (l)(n) "Public building" means any building owned or operated by:
10 (1) The state, including any branch, department, agency, bureau,
11 commission, authority or other instrumentality thereof;
12 (2) any county, city, township, other political subdivision, including
13 any commission, authority, agency or instrumentality thereof; or
14 (3) any other separate corporate instrumentality or unit of the state or
15 any municipality.
16 (m)(o) "Public meeting" means any meeting open to the public
17 pursuant to K.S.A. 75-4317 et seq., and amendments thereto, or any other
18 law of this state.
19 (n)(p) "Public place" means any enclosed areas open to the public or
20 used by the general public including, but not limited to: Banks, bars, food
21 service establishments, retail service establishments, retail stores, public
22 means of mass transportation, passenger elevators, health care institutions
23 or any other place where health care services are provided to the public,
24 medical care facilities, educational facilities, libraries, courtrooms, public
25 buildings, restrooms, grocery stores, school buses, museums, theaters,
26 auditoriums, arenas and recreational facilities. For purposes of this section,
27 a private residence shall not be considered a "public place" unless such
28 residence is used as a day care home, as defined in K.S.A. 65-530, and
29 amendments thereto.
30 (o)(q) "Smoking" means:
31 (1) Possession of a lighted cigarette, cigar, pipe or burning tobacco in
32 any other form or device designed for the use of tobacco;
33 (2) the use of an electronic cigarette; or
34 (3) burning marijuana in any form or device designed for the use of
35 marijuana.
36 (p)(r) "Tobacco shop" means any indoor area operated primarily for
37 the retail sale of tobacco, tobacco products or smoking devices or
38 accessories, and that derives not less than 65% of its gross receipts from
39 the sale of tobacco.
40 (q)(s) "Substantial dues or membership fee requirements" means
41 initiation costs, dues or fees proportional to the cost of membership in
42 similarly-situated outdoor recreational facilities that are not considered
43 nominal and implemented to otherwise avoid or evade restrictions of a
HB 2252 4
1 statewide ban on smoking.
2 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 21-6110 is hereby amended to read as follows: 21-
3 6110. (a) It shall be unlawful, with no requirement of a culpable mental
4 state, to smoke in an enclosed area or at a public meeting including, but
5 not limited to:
6 (1) Public places;
7 (2) taxicabs and limousines;
8 (3) restrooms, lobbies, hallways and other common areas in public
9 and private buildings, condominiums and other multiple-residential
10 facilities;
11 (4) restrooms, lobbies and other common areas in hotels and motels
12 and in at least 80% of the sleeping quarters within a hotel or motel that
13 may be rented to guests;
14 (5) access points of all buildings and facilities not exempted pursuant
15 to subsection (d); and
16 (6) any place of employment.
17 (b) Each employer having a place of employment that is an enclosed
18 area shall provide a smoke-free workplace for all employees. Such
19 employer shall also adopt and maintain a written smoking policy which
20 shall prohibit smoking without exception in all areas of the place of
21 employment. Such policy shall be communicated to all current employees
22 within one week of its adoption and shall be communicated to all new
23 employees upon hiring. Each employer shall provide a written copy of the
24 smoking policy upon request to any current or prospective employee.
25 (c) Notwithstanding any other provision of this section, K.S.A. 21-
26 6111 or 21-6112, and amendments thereto, the proprietor or other person
27 in charge of an adult care home, as defined in K.S.A. 39-923, and
28 amendments thereto, or a medical care facility, may designate a portion of
29 such adult care home, or the licensed long-term care unit of such medical
30 care facility, as a smoking area, and smoking may be permitted within such
31 designated smoking area.
32 (d) The provisions of this section shall not apply to:
33 (1) The outdoor areas of any building or facility beyond the access
34 points of such building or facility;
35 (2) private homes or residences, except when such home or residence
36 is used as a day care home, as defined in K.S.A. 65-530, and amendments
37 thereto;
38 (3) a hotel or motel room rented to one or more guests if the total
39 percentage of such hotel or motel rooms in such hotel or motel does not
40 exceed 20%;
41 (4) the gaming floor of a lottery gaming facility or racetrack gaming
42 facility, as those terms are defined in K.S.A. 74-8702, and amendments
43 thereto;
HB 2252 5
1 (5) that portion of an adult care home, as defined in K.S.A. 39-923,
2 and amendments thereto, that is expressly designated as a smoking area by
3 the proprietor or other person in charge of such adult care home pursuant
4 to subsection (c) and that is fully enclosed and ventilated;
5 (6) that portion of a licensed long-term care unit of a medical care
6 facility that is expressly designated as a smoking area by the proprietor or
7 other person in charge of such medical care facility pursuant to subsection
8 (c) and that is fully enclosed and ventilated and to which access is
9 restricted to the residents and their guests;
10 (7)(4) tobacco shops; and
11 (8)(5) a class A or class B club defined in K.S.A. 41-2601, and
12 amendments thereto, which: (A) Held a license pursuant to K.S.A. 41-
13 2606 et seq., and amendments thereto, as of January 1, 2009; and (B)
14 notifies the secretary of health and environment in writing, not later than
15 90 days after the effective date of this act September 29, 2010, that it
16 wishes to continue to allow smoking on its premises;
17 (9) a private club in designated areas where minors are prohibited;
18 (10) any benefit cigar dinner or other cigar dinner of a substantially
19 similar nature that:(A) Is conducted specifically and exclusively for
20 charitable purposes by a nonprofit organization which is exempt from
21 federal income taxation pursuant to section 501(c)(3) of the federal
22 internal revenue code of 1986;(B) is conducted no more than once per
23 calendar year by such organization; and(C) has been held during each of
24 the previous three years prior to January 1, 2011; and
25 (11) that portion of a medical or clinical research facility constituting
26 a separately ventilated, secure smoking room dedicated and used solely
27 and exclusively for clinical research activities conducted in accordance
28 with regulatory authority of the United States or the state of Kansas, as
29 determined by the director of alcoholic beverage control of the department
30 of revenue.
31 Sec. 3. K.S.A. 21-6109 and 21-6110 are hereby repealed.
32 Sec. 4. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
33 publication in the statute book.
