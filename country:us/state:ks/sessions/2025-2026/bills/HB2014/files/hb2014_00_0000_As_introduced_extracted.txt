Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Taxation
Requested by Representative Fairchild
1-15
1 AN ACT concerning property taxation; relating to exemptions; providing
2 exemptions for certain personal property including watercraft, marine
3 equipment, off-road vehicles, motorized bicycles and certain trailers;
4 amending K.S.A. 79-213 and 79-5501 and repealing the existing
5 sections.
6
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
8 New Section 1. (a) The following described property, to the extent
9 herein specified, is hereby exempt from all property or ad valorem taxes
10 levied under the laws of the state of Kansas:
11 (1) Any off-road vehicle that is not operated upon any highway;
12 (2) any motorized bicycle, electric-assisted bicycle, electric-assisted
13 scooter, electric personal assistive mobility device and motorized
14 wheelchair as such terms are defined in K.S.A. 8-126, and amendments
15 thereto;
16 (3) any trailer having a gross weight of 15,000 pounds or less that is
17 used exclusively for personal use and not for the production of income;
18 and
19 (4) any marine equipment.
20 (b) For purposes of this section:
21 (1) "Marine equipment" means any watercraft trailer designed to
22 launch, retrieve, transport and store watercraft and any watercraft motor
23 designed to operate watercraft on the water;
24 (2) "off-road motorcycle" means any motorcycle as defined in K.S.A.
25 8-126, and amendments thereto, that has been manufactured for off-road
26 use only and is used exclusively off roads and highways; and
27 (3) "off-road vehicle" means:
28 (A) Any all-terrain vehicle, recreational off-highway vehicle and golf
29 cart as such terms are defined in K.S.A. 8-126, and amendments thereto;
30 and
31 (B) any off-road motorcycle and snowmobile.
32 (c) The provisions of this section shall apply to all taxable years
33 commencing after December 31, 2025.
34 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 79-213 is hereby amended to read as follows: 79-213.
35 (a) Any property owner requesting an exemption from the payment of ad
HB 2014 2
1 valorem property taxes assessed, or to be assessed, against their property
2 shall be required to file an initial request for exemption, on forms
3 approved by the state board of tax appeals and provided by the county
4 appraiser.
5 (b) The initial exemption request shall identify the property for which
6 the exemption is requested and state, in detail, the legal and factual basis
7 for the exemption claimed.
8 (c) The request for exemption shall be filed with the county appraiser
9 of the county where such property is principally located.
10 (d) After a review of the exemption request, and after a preliminary
11 examination of the facts as alleged, the county appraiser shall recommend
12 that the exemption request either be granted or denied, and, if necessary,
13 that a hearing be held. If a denial is recommended, a statement of the
14 controlling facts and law relied upon shall be included on the form.
15 (e) The county appraiser, after making such written recommendation,
16 shall file the request for exemption and the recommendations of the county
17 appraiser with the state board of tax appeals. With regard to a request for
18 exemption from property tax pursuant to the provisions of K.S.A. 79-201g
19 and 82a-409, and amendments thereto, not filed with the board of tax
20 appeals by the county appraiser on or before the effective date of this act,
21 if the county appraiser recommends the exemption request be granted, the
22 exemption shall be provided in the amount recommended by the county
23 appraiser and the county appraiser shall not file the request for exemption
24 and recommendations of the county appraiser with the state board of tax
25 appeals. The county clerk or county assessor shall annually make such
26 adjustment in the taxes levied against the real property as the owner may
27 be entitled to receive under the provisions of K.S.A. 79-201g, and
28 amendments thereto, as recommended by the county appraiser, beginning
29 with the first period, following the date of issue of the certificate of
30 completion on which taxes are regularly levied, and during the years
31 which the landowner is entitled to such adjustment.
32 (f) Upon receipt of the request for exemption, the board shall docket
33 the same and notify the applicant and the county appraiser of such fact.
34 (g) After examination of the request for exemption and the county
35 appraiser's recommendation related thereto, the board may fix a time and
36 place for hearing, and shall notify the applicant and the county appraiser of
37 the time and place so fixed. A request for exemption pursuant to: (1)
38 Section 13 of article 11 of the constitution of the state of Kansas; or (2)
39 K.S.A. 79-201a Second, and amendments thereto, for property constructed
40 or purchased, in whole or in part, with the proceeds of revenue bonds
41 under the authority of K.S.A. 12-1740 through 12-1749, and amendments
42 thereto, prepared in accordance with instructions and assistance which
43 shall be provided by the department of commerce, shall be deemed
HB 2014 3
1 approved unless scheduled for hearing within 30 days after the date of
2 receipt of all required information and data relating to the request for
3 exemption, and such hearing shall be conducted within 90 days after such
4 date. Such time periods shall be determined without regard to any
5 extension or continuance allowed to either party to such request. In any
6 case where a party to such request for exemption requests a hearing
7 thereon, the same shall be granted. Hearings shall be conducted in
8 accordance with the provisions of the Kansas administrative procedure act.
9 In all instances where the board sets a request for exemption for hearing,
10 the county shall be represented by its county attorney or county counselor.
11 (h) Except as otherwise provided by subsection (g), in the event of a
12 hearing, the same shall be originally set not later than 90 days after the
13 filing of the request for exemption with the board.
14 (i) During the pendency of a request for exemption, no person, firm,
15 unincorporated association, company or corporation charged with real
16 estate or personal property taxes pursuant to K.S.A. 79-2004 and 79-
17 2004a, and amendments thereto, on the tax books in the hands of the
18 county treasurer shall be required to pay the tax from the date the request
19 is filed with the county appraiser until the expiration of 30 days after the
20 board issued its order thereon and the same becomes a final order. In the
21 event that taxes have been assessed against the subject property, no interest
22 shall accrue on any unpaid tax for the year or years in question nor shall
23 the unpaid tax be considered delinquent from the date the request is filed
24 with the county appraiser until the expiration of 30 days after the board
25 issued its order thereon. In the event the board determines an application
26 for exemption is without merit and filed in bad faith to delay the due date
27 of the tax, the tax shall be considered delinquent as of the date the tax
28 would have been due pursuant to K.S.A. 79-2004 and 79-2004a, and
29 amendments thereto, and interest shall accrue as prescribed therein.
30 (j) In the event the board grants the initial request for exemption, the
31 same shall be effective beginning with the date of first exempt use except
32 that, with respect to property the construction of which commenced not to
33 exceed 24 months prior to the date of first exempt use, the same shall be
34 effective beginning with the date of commencement of construction.
35 (k) In conjunction with its authority to grant exemptions, the board
36 shall have the authority to abate all unpaid taxes that have accrued from
37 and since the effective date of the exemption. In the event that taxes have
38 been paid during the period where the subject property has been
39 determined to be exempt, the board shall have the authority to order a
40 refund of taxes for the year immediately preceding the year in which the
41 exemption application is filed in accordance with subsection (a).
42 (l) The provisions of this section shall not apply to: (1) Farm
43 machinery and equipment exempted from ad valorem taxation by K.S.A.

36 Sec. 3. K.S.A. 79-5501 is hereby amended to read as follows: 79-
37 5501. (a) On and after Commencing July 1, 2013, and through December
38 31, 2025, watercraft shall be appraised at fair market value determined
39 therefor pursuant to K.S.A. 79-503a, and amendments thereto, and
40 assessed at the percentage of value as follows: (1) 11.5% in tax year 2014;
41 and (2) 5% in tax year years 2015 and all tax years thereafter through
42 2025. On and after January 1, 2014, the levy used to calculate the tax on
43 watercraft shall be the county average tax rate. In no case shall the
HB 2014 6
1 assessed value of any watercraft, as determined under the provisions of
2 this section, cause the tax upon such watercraft to be less than $12.
3 (b) As used in this section, the term "watercraft" means any
4 watercraft designed to be propelled by machinery, oars, paddles or wind
5 action upon a sail for navigation on the water which, if not for the
6 provisions of this section, would be properly classified under subclass 5 or
7 6 of class 2 of section 1 of article 11 of the Kansas constitution. This
8 section shall not be construed as taxing any watercraft which otherwise
9 would be exempt from property taxation under the laws of the state of
10 Kansas. Each watercraft may include one trailer which is designed to
11 launch, retrieve, transport and store such watercraft and any nonelectric
12 motor or motors which are necessary to operate such watercraft on the
13 water.
14 (c) Any watercraft which is designed to be propelled through the
15 water through human power alone shall be exempt from all property or ad
16 valorem taxes levied under the laws of the state of Kansas.
17 (d) The "county average tax rate" means the total amount of general
18 property taxes levied within the county by the state, county and all other
19 taxing subdivisions divided by the total assessed valuation of all taxable
20 property within the county as of November 1 of the year prior to the year
21 of valuation as certified by the secretary of revenue.
22 (e) On and after January 1, 2026, all watercraft shall be exempt from
23 all property or ad valorem taxes levied under the laws of the state of
24 Kansas.
25 Sec. 4. K.S.A. 79-213 and 79-5501 are hereby repealed.
26 Sec. 5. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
27 publication in the statute book.
