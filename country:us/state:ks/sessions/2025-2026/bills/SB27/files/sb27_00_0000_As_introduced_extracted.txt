Section 1:
Session of 2025
By Committee on Financial Institutions and Insurance
1-16
1 AN ACT concerning insurance; relating to the powers, duties and
2 responsibilities of the commissioner; eliminating the requirement that
3 the commissioner submit certain reports to the governor; removing
4 certain specific entities from the definition of person for the purpose of
5 enforcing insurance law; amending K.S.A. 40-108 and 40-2253 and
6 K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 40-2,125 and repealing the existing sections.
7
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
9 Section 1. K.S.A. 40-108 is hereby amended to read as follows: 40-
10 108. The commissioner of insurance shall make an annual report to the
11 governor of the general conduct and condition of the insurance companies,
12 including fraternal benefit societies, doing business in this state. The
13 commissioner of insurance shall keep and preserve in a permanent form a
14 full record of the commissioner's proceedings, including a concise
15 statement of the condition of each company reported, visited or examined
16 by the commissioner.
17 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 40-2,125 is hereby amended to read as
18 follows: 40-2,125. (a) If the commissioner determines after notice and
19 opportunity for a hearing that any person has engaged or is engaging in
20 any act or practice constituting a violation of any provision of Kansas
21 insurance statutes or any rule and regulation or order thereunder, the
22 commissioner may, in the exercise of discretion, order any one or more of
23 the following:
24 (1) Payment of a monetary penalty of not more than $1,000 for each
25 and every act or violation, unless the person knew or reasonably should
26 have known that such person was in violation of the Kansas insurance
27 statutes or any rule and regulation or order thereunder, in which case the
28 penalty shall be not more than $2,000 for each and every act or violation;
29 (2) suspension or revocation of the person's license or certificate if
30 such person knew or reasonably should have known that such person was
31 in violation of the Kansas insurance statutes or any rule and regulation or
32 order thereunder; or
33 (3) that such person cease and desist from the unlawful act or practice
34 and take such affirmative action as that, in the judgment of the
35 commissioner, will carry out the purposes of the violated or potentially
36 violated provision.
SB 27 2
1 (b) If any person fails to file any report or other information with the
2 commissioner as required by statute or fails to respond to any proper
3 inquiry of the commissioner, the commissioner, after notice and
4 opportunity for hearing, may impose a civil penalty of up to $1,000, for
5 each violation or act, along with an additional penalty of up to $500 for
6 each week thereafter that such report or other information is not provided
7 to the commissioner.
8 (c) If the commissioner makes written findings of fact that there is a
9 situation involving an immediate danger to the public health, safety or
10 welfare or the public interest will be irreparably harmed by delay in
11 issuing an order under subsection (a)(3), the commissioner may issue an
12 emergency temporary cease and desist order. Such order, even when not an
13 order within the meaning of K.S.A. 77-502, and amendments thereto, shall
14 be subject to the same procedures as an emergency order issued under
15 K.S.A. 77-536, and amendments thereto. Upon the entry of such an order,
16 the commissioner shall promptly notify the person subject to the order
17 that: (1) It Such order has been entered; (2) the reasons therefor; and (3)
18 that upon written request within 15 days after service of the order, the
19 matter will be set for a hearing, which shall be conducted in accordance
20 with the provisions of the Kansas administrative procedure act. If no
21 hearing is requested and none is ordered by the commissioner, the order
22 will remain in effect until it is modified or vacated by the commissioner. If
23 a hearing is requested or ordered, the commissioner, after notice of and
24 opportunity for hearing to the person subject to the order, shall, by written
25 findings of fact and conclusions of law vacate, modify or make permanent
26 the order.
27 (d) For purposes of this section:
28 (1) "Person" means any individual, corporation, association,
29 partnership, reciprocal exchange, inter-insurer, Lloyd's insurer, fraternal
30 benefit society and any other legal entity engaged in the business of
31 insurance, rating organization, third party administrator, nonprofit dental
32 service corporation, nonprofit medical and hospital service corporation,
33 automobile club, premium financing company, health maintenance
34 organization, insurance holding company, mortgage guaranty insurance
35 company, risk retention or purchasing group, prepaid legal and dental
36 service plan, captive insurance company, automobile self-insurer or
37 reinsurance intermediary and any other legal entity under the jurisdiction
38 of the commissioner. The term "person" does not include insurance agents
39 and brokers as such terms are defined in K.S.A. 40-4902, and amendments
40 thereto.
41 (2) "Commissioner" means the commissioner of insurance of this
42 state.
43 Sec. 3. K.S.A. 40-2253 is hereby amended to read as follows: 40-
SB 27 3
1 2253. (a) The commissioner of insurance shall devise universal forms to be
2 utilized by every insurance company, including health maintenance
3 organizations where applicable, offering any type of accident and sickness
4 policy covering individuals residing in this state for the purpose of
5 receiving every claim under such policy by persons covered thereunder. In
6 the preparation of such forms, the commissioner may confer with
7 representatives of insurance companies, health maintenance organizations,
8 trade associations and other interested parties. Upon completion and final
9 adoption of such forms by the commissioner, the commissioner shall
10 notify those companies affected by sending them a copy of such forms and
11 an explanation of the requirements of this section. Every such company
12 shall implement utilization of such forms not later than six months
13 following the date of the commissioner's notification.
14 (b) An accident and sickness insurer may not refuse to accept a claim
15 submitted on duly promulgated uniform claim forms. An insurer may
16 accept claims submitted on any other form.
17 (c) An accident and sickness insurer does not violate subsection (a)
18 by using a document that the accident and sickness insurer has been
19 required to use by the federal government or the state.
20 (d) The commissioner of insurance shall report to the governor and to
21 the legislature, no later than the commencement of the 1993 regular
22 session of the Kansas legislature, regarding the development of uniform
23 electronic data interchange formats and standards, along with a proposed
24 plan, including an analysis of the cost impact thereof.
25 Sec. 4. K.S.A. 40-108 and 40-2253 and K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 40-2,125
26 are hereby repealed.
27 Sec. 5. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
28 publication in the statute book.
