1 AN ACT concerning the Kansas offender registration act; making it
2 unlawful for certain adult sex offenders to enter onto certain school
3 property or attend certain school activities and creating criminal
4 penalties for violation thereof; prohibiting certain adult sex offenders
5 from residing within 1,000 feet of any school property; authorizing
6 certain offenders to petition for relief from registration
7 requirements; amending K.S.A. 22-4901 and, 22-4902 and 22-4908
8 and repealing the existing sections.

10 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
11 New Section 1. (a) (1) Except as provided in paragraphs (2), (3)
12 and (4), it shall be unlawful for any sex offender who is 18 years of age or
13 older and whose underlying crime for which the offender is required to
14 register under the Kansas offender registration act was a crime against a
15 victim who was less than 18 16 years of age to enter onto school property
16 or attend a school activity.
17 (2) The provisions of this subsection shall not apply to a person
18 who enters onto school property for the sole purpose of:
19 (A) Voting in an election when such school property is being used
20 as a polling place and the person leaves such school property
21 immediately after voting; or
22 (B) attending a religious service when such school property is
23 being used for a religious service and the person leaves such school
24 property immediately after such religious service.
25 (3) The provisions of this subsection shall not apply to a person
26 who enters onto school property for instruction or attendance when
27 the person is a student enrolled at a school in the unified school
28 district that is using such property or at an accredited nonpublic
29 school that is using such property. Such school district or school may
30 take actions as necessary to protect the safety of students and school
31 employees.
32 (4) The provisions of this subsection shall not apply if such
33 offender is a parent or guardian of an enrolled student and complies
34 with any procedures established by the local school board to monitor
35 the whereabouts of the offender for the duration of such offender's

3 publish such procedures on the board's public website and include a

8 (1) Upon a first conviction, a severity level 6 8, person felony;

14 New Sec. 2. (a) Except as provided in subsection (b), a sex
15 offender who is 18 years of age or older and required to register for
16 such offender's lifetime and whose underlying crime for which the
17 offender is required to register under the Kansas offender registration
18 act was a crime against a victim who was less than 18 years of age
19 shall not reside within 1,000 feet of any school property.
20 (b) A sex offender described in subsection (a) may reside within
21 1,000 feet of a school property if such offender:
22 (1) Resides in a residence prior to July 1, 2025, that is within
23 1,000 feet of a school property. Such offender shall, prior to July 31,
24 2025, notify the registering law enforcement agency of the county or
25 location of jurisdiction where such school property is located that such
26 offender resides within 1,000 feet of such school property and provide
27 verifiable proof to such agency that such offender resided in such
28 residence prior to July 1, 2025; or
29 (2) resides in a residence that was not within 1,000 feet of a school
30 property but school property has been subsequently built or acquired
31 within 1,000 feet of such residence. Not later than 7 days after such
32 school property begins being used for instruction of students, such
33 offender shall notify the registering law enforcement agency of the
34 county or location of jurisdiction where such school property is
35 located that such offender resides within 1,000 feet of such school
36 property and provide verifiable proof to such agency that such
37 offender resided in such residence prior to such school property being
38 used for instruction of students.
39 (c) For the purposes of this section, 1,000 feet shall be measured
40 from the edge of the structure in which the offender resides, including
41 an attached garage, that is nearest to the school property to the edge
42 of the school property that is nearest to such structure.
