Section 1:
As Amended by Senate Committee
Session of 2025
By Committee on Financial Institutions and Insurance
1-16
1 AN ACT concerning insurance; relating to licensing requirements for
2 insurance agents and public adjusters; pertaining to suspension,
3 revocation, denial of licensure and licensure renewal; amending K.S.A.
4 40-5510 and K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 40-4909 and repealing the existing
5 sections.
6
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
8 Section 1. K.S.A. 2024 Supp. 40-4909 is hereby amended to read as
9 follows: 40-4909. (a) The commissioner may deny, suspend, revoke or
10 refuse renewal of any license issued under this act if the commissioner
11 finds that the applicant or license holder has:
12 (1) Provided incorrect, misleading, incomplete or untrue information
13 in the license application.
14 (2) Violated:
15 (A) Any provision of chapter 40 of the Kansas Statutes Annotated,
16 and amendments thereto, or any rules and regulations promulgated
17 thereunder;
18 (B) any subpoena or order of the commissioner;
19 (C) any insurance law or regulation of another state; or
20 (D) any subpoena or order issued by the regulatory official for
21 insurance in another state.
22 (3) Obtained or attempted to obtain a license under this act through
23 misrepresentation or fraud.
24 (4) Improperly withheld, misappropriated or converted any moneys
25 or properties received in the course of doing insurance business.
26 (5) Intentionally misrepresented the provisions, terms and conditions
27 of an actual or proposed insurance contract or application for insurance.
28 (6) Been convicted of a misdemeanor or felony.
29 (7) Admitted to or been found to have committed any insurance
30 unfair trade practice or fraud in violation of K.S.A. 40-2404, and
31 amendments thereto.
32 (8) Used any fraudulent, coercive, or dishonest practice, or
33 demonstrated any incompetence, untrustworthiness or financial
34 irresponsibility in the conduct of business in this state or elsewhere.
35 (9) Had an insurance agent license, public adjuster license, securities
36 registration, or its their equivalent, denied, suspended or revoked in any
SB 28—Am. by SC 2
1 state, district or territory.
2 (10) Forged another person's name to an application for insurance or
3 to any document related to an insurance transaction.
4 (11) Improperly used notes or any other reference material to
5 complete an examination for an insurance license issued under this act.
6 (12) Knowingly accepted insurance business from an individual who
7 is not licensed.
8 (13) Failed to comply with any administrative or court order
9 imposing a child support obligation upon the applicant or license holder.
10 (14) Failed to pay any state income tax or comply with any
11 administrative or court order directing payment of state income tax.
12 (15) Except as otherwise permitted by law, rebated the whole or any
13 part of any insurance premium or offered in connection with the
14 presentation of any contract of insurance any other inducement not
15 contained in the contract of insurance.
16 (16) Made any misleading representation or incomplete comparison
17 of policies to any person for the purposes of inducing or tending to induce
18 such person to lapse, forfeit or surrender such person's insurance then in
19 force.
20 (17) Failed to respond to an inquiry from the commissioner within 15
21 business days.
22 (b) In addition, the commissioner may deny, suspend, revoke or
23 refuse renewal of any license issued under this act if the commissioner
24 finds that the interests of the insurer or the insurable interests of the public
25 are not properly served under such license.
26 (c) (1) When considering whether to deny, suspend, revoke or refuse
27 to renew the application of an individual who has been convicted of a
28 misdemeanor or felony, the commissioner shall consider the:
29 (A) Applicant's age at the time of the conduct;
30 (B) recency of the conduct;
31 (C) reliability of the information concerning the conduct;
32 (D) seriousness of the conduct;
33 (E) factors underlying the conduct;
34 (F) cumulative effect of the conduct or information;
35 (G) evidence of rehabilitation;
36 (H) applicant's social contributions since the conduct;
37 (I) applicant's candor in the application process; and
38 (J) materiality of any omissions or misrepresentations.
39 (2) In determining whether to reinstate or grant to an applicant a
40 license that has been revoked, the commissioner shall consider the:
41 (A) Present moral fitness of the applicant;
42 (B) demonstrated consciousness by the applicant of the wrongful
43 conduct and disrepute that the conduct has brought to the insurance
SB 28—Am. by SC 3
1 profession;
2 (C) extent of the applicant's rehabilitation;
3 (D) seriousness of the original conduct;
4 (E) applicant's conduct subsequent to discipline;
5 (F) amount of time that has elapsed since the original discipline;
6 (G) applicant's character, maturity and experience at the time of
7 revocation; and
8 (H) applicant's present competence and skills in the insurance
9 industry.
10 (d) Any action taken under this section that affects any license or
11 imposes any administrative penalty shall be taken only after notice and an
12 opportunity for a hearing conducted in accordance with the provisions of
13 the Kansas administrative procedure act.
14 (e) The license of any business entity may be suspended, revoked or
15 refused renewal if the insurance commissioner finds that any violation
16 committed by an individual licensee employed by or acting on behalf of
17 such business entity was known by or should have been known by one or
18 more of the partners, officers or managers acting on behalf of the business
19 entity and:
20 (1) Such violation was not reported to the insurance commissioner by
21 such business entity; or
22 (2) such business entity failed to take any corrective action.
23 (f) None of the following actions shall deprive the commissioner of
24 any jurisdiction or right to institute or proceed with any disciplinary
25 proceeding against such license, to render a decision suspending, revoking
26 or refusing to renew such license, or to establish and make a record of the
27 facts of any violation of law for any lawful purpose:
28 (1) The imposition of an administrative penalty under this section;
29 (2) the lapse or suspension of any license issued under this act by
30 operation of law;
31 (3) the licensee's failure to renew any license issued under this act; or
32 (4) the licensee's voluntary surrender of any license issued under this
33 act. No such disciplinary proceeding shall be instituted against any
34 licensee after the expiration of two years from the termination of the
35 license.
36 (g) Whenever the commissioner imposes any administrative penalty
37 or denies, suspends, revokes or refuses renewal of any license pursuant to
38 subsection (a), any costs incurred as a result of conducting an
39 administrative hearing authorized under the provisions of this section shall
40 be assessed against the person who is the subject of the hearing or any
41 business entity represented by such person who is the party to the matters
42 giving rise to the hearing. As used in this subsection, "costs" shall include
43 includes witness fees, mileage allowances, any costs associated with the
SB 28—Am. by SC 4
1 reproduction of documents that become a part of the hearing record and
2 the expense of making a record of the hearing.
3 (h) No person whose license as an agent or broker had been
4 suspended or revoked shall be employed by any insurance company doing
5 business in this state either directly, indirectly, as an independent
6 contractor or otherwise to negotiate or effect contracts of insurance,
7 suretyship or indemnity or perform any act toward the solicitation of or
8 transaction of any business of insurance during the period of such
9 suspension or revocation.
10 (i) In lieu of taking any action under subsection (a), the commissioner
11 may:
12 (1) Censure the person; or
13 (2) issue an order imposing an administrative penalty up to a
14 maximum of $500 for each violation but not to exceed $2,500 for the same
15 violation occurring within any six consecutive calendar months from the
16 date of the original violation unless such person knew or should have
17 known that the violative act could give rise to disciplinary action under
18 subsection (a). If such person knew or reasonably should have known the
19 violative act could give rise to any disciplinary proceeding authorized by
20 subsection (a), the commissioner may impose a penalty up to a maximum
21 of $1,000 for each violation but not to exceed $5,000 for the same
22 violation occurring within any six consecutive calendar months from the
23 date of the imposition of the original administrative penalty.
24 (j) (1) An applicant to whom a license has been denied after a hearing
25 shall not apply again for a license again until after the expiration of a
26 period of one year from the date of the commissioner's order.
27 (2) A licensee whose license was revoked shall not apply again for a
28 license again until after the expiration of a period of two years from the
29 date of the commissioner's order.
