Section 1:
As Amended by House Committee
Session of 2025
By Committee on Judiciary
1-27
1 AN ACT concerning crimes, punishment and criminal procedure; relating
2 to crimes involving property; modifying criminal use of a financial card
3 to include certain conduct involving gift cards; amending K.S.A. 21-
4 5828 and repealing the existing section.
5
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Kansas:
7 Section 1. K.S.A. 21-5828 is hereby amended to read as follows: 21-
8 5828. (a) Criminal use of a financial card or gift card is any of the
9 following acts done with intent to defraud and to obtain money, goods,
10 property or services:
11 (1) Using a financial card without the consent of the cardholder;
12 (2) using a financial card, or the number or description thereof, which
13 has been revoked or canceled; or
14 (3) using a falsified, mutilated, altered or nonexistent financial card or
15 a number or description thereof;
16 (4) acquiring or retaining possession of a gift card or gift card
17 redemption information without the consent of the cardholder, gift card
18 issuer or gift card seller; or
19 (5) altering or tampering with a gift card.
20 (b) Criminal use of a financial card or gift card is a:
21 (1) Severity level 7, nonperson felony if the money, goods, property
22 or services obtained within any seven-day period are of the value of
23 $25,000 or more;
24 (2) Severity level 9, nonperson felony if the money, goods, property
25 or services obtained within any seven-day period are of the value of at
26 least $1,000 but less than $25,000; and
27 (3) class A nonperson misdemeanor if the money, goods, property or
28 services obtained within a seven-day period are of the value of less than
29 $1,000.
30 (c) As used in this section:
31 (1) "Cardholder" means:
32 (A) The person or entity to whom or for whose benefit a financial
33 card is issued; or
34 (B) the person to whom a physical or virtual gift card is issued or any
35 person who has agreed with the card issuer to pay obligations arising
36 from the issuance of a gift card to another person;
SB 84—Am. by HC 2
1 (2) "closed-loop gift card" means a card, code or device that is
2 issued to a consumer on a prepaid basis primarily for personal, family or
3 household purposes in a specified amount, regardless of whether that
4 amount may be increased or reloaded in exchange for payment, and is
5 redeemable upon presentation by a consumer at a single merchant or
6 group of affiliated merchants;
7 (3) "financial card" means an identification card, plate, instrument,
8 device or number issued by a business organization authorizing the
9 cardholder to purchase, lease or otherwise obtain money, goods, property
10 or services or to conduct other financial transactions; and
11 (2) "cardholder" means the person or entity to whom or for whose
12 benefit a financial card is issued
13 (4) "gift card" means a physical or digital closed-loop gift card or
14 open-loop gift card that is either activated or inactivated;
15 (5) "gift card issuer" means any person that issues a gift card or the
16 agent of that person with respect to such card;
17 (6) "gift card redemption information" means information unique to
18 each gift card that allows the cardholder to access, transfer or spend the
19 funds on such gift card;
20 (7) "gift card seller" means a merchant that is engaged in the
21 business of selling gift cards to consumers; and
22 (8) "open-loop gift card" means a card, code or device that is issued
23 to a consumer on a prepaid basis primarily for personal, family or
24 household purposes in a specified amount, regardless of whether that
25 amount may be increased or reloaded in exchange for payment, and is
26 redeemable upon presentation at multiple unaffiliated merchants for goods
27 or services within the payment card network.
28 (d) For the purposes of subsection (a)(2), a financial card shall be
29 deemed canceled or revoked when notice in writing thereof has been
30 received by the named holder thereof as shown on such financial card or
31 by the records of the company.
32 Sec. 2. K.S.A. 21-5828 is hereby repealed.
33 Sec. 3. This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its
34 publication in the statute book Kansas register.
